    14)    !=======================================================================================!
    15)    !     The following subroutine calculates the turbulent transfer of scalar quantities   !
    16)    ! using Monin Obukhov Similarity Theory under a few different flavors.  The basic       !
    17)    ! assumptions are that the ground surface and canopy space is modeled as a rough wall   !
    18)    ! boundary above which exists a dynamic sublayer, above which exists the free atmo-     !
    19)    ! sphere.  We assume a logarithmic decay of wind speed over the dynamic sublayer, which !
    20)    ! is due to the assumption of constant shear (ustar) in the dynamic sublayer.           !
    21)    !                                                                                       !
    22)    ! 0.  We apply the wind profile based on the similarity theory for the tallest cohort,  !
    23)    !     with further reduction due to the presence of obstacles (trees), similar to       !
    24)    !     Leuning et al. (1995) but considering a finite crown area.  The aerodynamic       !
    25)    !     resistance from ground to canopy air is found in a similar way as in LEAF-3.      !
    26)    !     Displacement height and roughness are linear functions of the vegetation height,  !
    27)    !     where vegetation height is the weighted average of cohort heights, the weights    !
    28)    !     being the basal area.                                                             !
    29)    !     References:                                                                       !
    30)    !                                                                                       !
    31)    !     Leuning, R., F. M. Kelliher, D. G. G. de Pury, E. D. Schulze, 1995: Leaf          !
    32)    !       nitrogen, photosynthesis, conductance and transpiration: scaling from leaves to !
    33)    !       canopies.  Plant, Cell and Environ., 18, 1183-1200.                             !
    34)    !                                                                                       !
    35)    ! 1. This is similar to option 0, where displacement height and rougness are linear     !
    36)    !    functions of mean canopy height, where mean canopy height is defined by the        !
    37)    !    dead-biomass weighted average of canopy height.  Wind speed and diffusivity decay  !
    38)    !    exponentially in the canopy on parameter alpha.  HOWEVER, if the windspeed at      !
    39)    !    reference height is less than the canopy height (WHICH IS REALLY INNAPROPRIATE TO  !
    40)    !    BEGIN WITH, BUT OH WELL), then revert to a calculation of ustar that uses no       !
    41)    !    displacement height.  After ustar is calculated, then use displacement height in   !
    42)    !    the calculation of the eddy length scale at the canopy top to find diffusivity at  !
    43)    !    canopy top. THIS IS THE METHOD USED IN LEAF3 AND ED2.0.                            !
    44)    !                                                                                       !
    45)    ! 2. Follow the methods of Massman 1997.  The canopy top is taken as the height of the  !
    46)    !    tallest cohort in the patch. The displacement height is determined by a center of  !
    47)    !    pressure method. The wind speed inside the canopy decays exponentially on          !
    48)    !    cumulative foliar drag elements, where the equation is scaled such that it solves  !
    49)    !    for the balance of integrated canopy shear with dissipation.  To close the         !
    50)    !    equations M97 assumes that the surface drag (U(h)/Ustar) is a function of the      !
    51)    !    cumulative foliar drag surfaces.  With this, we can explicitly solve for roughness !
    52)    !    length. Assumptions are made about the profile of canopy drag as a function of     !
    53)    !    cohort attributes, please see the technical manual by RGK2009 for more info.       !
    54)    !    Please also refer to the original paper by Massman for information regarding the   !
    55)    !    basic principles of the closure scheme.                                            !
    56)    !                                                                                       !
    57)    !                                                                                       !
    58)    !    Massman, W.J., 1997: An analytical one-Dimensional model of momentum transfer by   !
    59)    !        vegetation of arbitrary structure. Boundary-Layer Meteorol., 83, 407-421.      !
    60)    !    Wohlfahrt, G., and A. Cernusca, 2002: Momentum transfer by a mountain meadow       !
    61)    !        canopy: a simulation analysis based on Massman's (1997) model.  Boundary-Layer !
    62)    !        Meteorol., 103, 391-407.                                                       !
    63)    !                                                                                       !
    64)    ! 3.  This is related to option 2, but using a second-order clousure.                   !
    65)    !                                                                                       !
    66)    !    Massman, W. J., and J. C. Weil, 1999: An analytical one-dimension second-order     !
    67)    !        closure model turbulence statistics and the Lagrangian time scale within and   !
    68)    !        above plant canopies of arbitrary structure.  Boundary-Layer Meteorol., 91,    !
    69)    !        81-107.                                                                        !
    70)    !                                                                                       !
    71)    ! 4.  This option is almost the same as option 0, except that the ground conductance    !
    72)    !     beneath a vegetated canopy is found in a similar way as the CLM technical note,   !
    73)    !     equations (5.98-5.100).  The conductance for bare ground is still found the same  !
    74)    !     way as in the similarity theory.                                                  !
    75)    !                                                                                       !
    76)    !     Oleson, K. W., et al.; Technical description of version 4.0 of the community land !
    77)    !        model (CLM) NCAR Technical Note NCAR/TN-478+STR, Boulder, CO, April 2010.      !
    78)    !                                                                                       !
    79)    !     Ultimately, this routine solves for the resistance of water vapor and sensible    !
    80)    ! heat from the soil surface to canopy air space and from the leaf surfaces to canopy   !
    81)    ! air space.                                                                            !
    82)    !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
    83)    subroutine canopy_turbulence(cpoly,isi,ipa)
    84)       use ed_state_vars    , only : polygontype          & ! structure
    85)                                   , sitetype             & ! structure
    86)                                   , patchtype            ! ! structure
    87)       use met_driver_coms  , only : met_driv_state       ! ! structure
    88)       use grid_coms        , only : nzg                  ! ! intent(in)
    89)       use rk4_coms         , only : ibranch_thermo       ! ! intent(in)
    90)       use canopy_air_coms  , only : icanturb             & ! intent(in), can. turb. scheme
    91)                                   , ugbmin               & ! intent(in)
    92)                                   , vh2vr                & ! intent(in)
    93)                                   , vh2dh                & ! intent(in)
    94)                                   , veg_height_min       & ! intent(in)
    95)                                   , exar                 & ! intent(in)
    96)                                   , cdrag0               & ! intent(in)
    97)                                   , cdrag1               & ! intent(in)
    98)                                   , cdrag2               & ! intent(in)
    99)                                   , cdrag3               & ! intent(in)
   100)                                   , c1_m97               & ! intent(in)
   101)                                   , c2_m97               & ! intent(in)
   102)                                   , c3_m97               & ! intent(in)
   103)                                   , kvwake               & ! intent(in)
   104)                                   , alpha_m97            & ! intent(in)
   105)                                   , alpha_mw99           & ! intent(in)
   106)                                   , infunc               & ! intent(in)
   107)                                   , gamma_mw99           & ! intent(in)
   108)                                   , nu_mw99              & ! intent(in)
   109)                                   , cs_dense0            & ! intent(in)
   110)                                   , gamma_clm4           & ! intent(in)
   111)                                   , tprandtl             & ! intent(in)
   112)                                   , dpsimdzeta           ! ! function
   113)       use canopy_layer_coms, only : ncanlyr              & ! intent(in)
   114)                                   , dzcan                & ! intent(in)
   115)                                   , zztop0i              & ! intent(in)
   116)                                   , ehgti                & ! intent(in)
   117)                                   , zztop                & ! intent(in)
   118)                                   , zzbot                & ! intent(in)
   119)                                   , zzmid                & ! intent(in)
   120)                                   , zero_canopy_layer    & ! subroutine
   121)                                   , canstr
   122)       use consts_coms      , only : vonk                 & ! intent(in)
   123)                                   , grav                 & ! intent(in)
   124)                                   , t00                  & ! intent(in)
   125)                                   , epim1                & ! intent(in)
   126)                                   , sqrt2o2              & ! intent(in)
   127)                                   , srthree              & ! intent(in)
   128)                                   , onethird             & ! intent(in)
   129)                                   , twothirds            & ! intent(in)
   130)                                   , th_diff0             & ! intent(in)
   131)                                   , dth_diff             & ! intent(in)
   132)                                   , cpdry                & ! intent(in)
   133)                                   , cph2o                & ! intent(in)
   134)                                   , lnexp_min            & ! intent(in)
   135)                                   , lnexp_max            ! ! intent(in)
   136)       use soil_coms        , only : snow_rough           & ! intent(in)
   137)                                   , soil_rough           ! ! intent(in)
   138)       use pft_coms         , only : is_grass             ! ! intent(in)
   139)       use therm_lib        , only : press2exner          & ! function
   140)                                   , extheta2temp         & ! function
   141)                                   , tq2enthalpy          ! ! function
   142)       use allometry        , only : h2crownbh            & ! function
   143)                                   , size2bl          ! ! function
   144)       use ed_misc_coms     , only : igrass               ! ! intent(in)
   145)       use phenology_coms   , only : elongf_min           ! ! intent(in)
   146)       !$ use omp_lib
   147) 
   148)       implicit none
   149)       !----- Arguments --------------------------------------------------------------------!
   150)       type(polygontype)   , target      :: cpoly         ! Current polygon
   151)       integer             , intent(in)  :: isi           ! Site index
   152)       integer             , intent(in)  :: ipa           ! Patch index
   153)       !----- Pointers ---------------------------------------------------------------------!
   154)       type(sitetype)      , pointer     :: csite         ! Current site
   155)       type(patchtype)     , pointer     :: cpatch        ! Current patch
   156)       type(met_driv_state), pointer     :: cmet          ! Current meteorology
   157)       !----- Local variables --------------------------------------------------------------!
   158)       integer        :: ico          ! Cohort loop
   159)       integer        :: ipft         ! PFT alias
   160)       integer        :: k            ! Elevation index
   161)       integer        :: ksn          ! Number of temporary sfc. water layers    [      ---]
   162)       integer        :: kk           ! Counter                                  [      ---]
   163)       integer        :: zcan         ! Index of canopy top elevation
   164)       integer        :: zels         ! Index of roughness height
   165)       integer        :: kafull       ! First layer fully occupied by crown
   166)       integer        :: kzfull       ! Last layer fully occupied by crown
   167)       integer        :: kapartial    ! First layer partially occupied by crown
   168)       integer        :: kzpartial    ! Last layer partially occupied by crown
   169)       integer        :: nalpha_fails ! Counter for number of failed attemps     [      ---]
   170)       logical        :: stable       ! Stable canopy air space
   171)       logical        :: acomp        ! Flag to check for convergence            [      T|F]
   172)       real           :: rasveg       ! Resistance of vegetated ground           [      s/m]
   173)       real           :: atm_thetav   ! Free atmosphere virtual potential temp.  [        K]
   174)       real           :: can_thetav   ! Canopy air space virtual potential temp. [        K]
   175)       real           :: atm_exn_zcan ! Atmospheric Exner function at can. depth [   J/kg/K]
   176)       real           :: atm_tmp_zcan ! Atmospheric temperature at can. depth    [        K]
   177)       real           :: can_enthalpy ! Canopy air space specific enthalpy.      [     J/kg]
   178)       real           :: atm_enthalpy ! Free atmosphere specific enthalpy.       [     J/kg]
   179)       real           :: can_cp       ! Canopy air space specific heat           [   J/kg/K]
   180)       real           :: ldga_bk      ! Cumulative leaf drag area                [      ---]
   181)       real           :: lyrhalf      ! Half the contrib. of this layer to zeta  [      1/m]
   182)       real           :: sigmakh      ! Kh coefficient at z=h                    [        m]
   183)       real           :: K_top        ! Diffusivity at canopy top z=h            [     m2/s]
   184)       real           :: Kdiff        ! Diffusivity                              [     m2/s]
   185)       real           :: surf_rough   ! Roughness length of the bare ground
   186)                                      !     at canopy bottom                     [        m]
   187)       real           :: uh           ! Wind speed at the canopy top (z=h)       [      m/s]
   188)       real           :: factv        ! Wind-dependent term for old rasveg
   189)       real           :: aux          ! Aux. variable
   190)       real           :: estar        ! Equivalent potential temperature         [        K]
   191)       real           :: wcapcan      ! Canopy air space water capacity          [    kg/m2]
   192)       real           :: hcapcan      ! Canopy air space enthalpy capacity       [     J/m2]
   193)       real           :: ccapcan      ! Canopy air space CO2 capacity            [   mol/m2]
   194)       real           :: wcapcani     ! Inverse of the guy above                 [    m2/kg]
   195)       real           :: hcapcani     ! Inverse of canopy air space enthalpy cap.[     m2/J]
   196)       real           :: ccapcani     ! Inverse of canopy air space CO2 capacity [   m2/mol]
   197)       real           :: ustarouh     ! The ratio of ustar over u(h)             [      ---]
   198)       real           :: nn           ! In-canopy wind attenuation scal. param.  [      ---]
   199)       real           :: waiuse       ! Wood area index                          [    m2/m2]
   200)       real           :: htopcrown    ! height at the top of the crown           [        m]
   201)       real           :: hmidcrown    ! Height at the middle of the crown        [        m]
   202)       real           :: hbotcrown    ! Height at the bottom of the crown        [        m]
   203)       real           :: htop         ! Height of the topmost layer              [        m]
   204)       real           :: zetatop      ! Dimensionless height at the topmost lyr. [      ---]
   205)       real           :: d0ohgt       ! d0/height                                [      ---]
   206)       real           :: z0ohgt       ! z0/height                                [      ---]
   207)       real           :: ladcohort    ! Leaf Area Density of this cohort         [    m2/m3]
   208)       real           :: extinct_half ! Wind extinction coefficient at half lyr  [      ---]
   209)       real           :: extinct_full ! Full Wind extinction coefficient         [      ---]
   210)       real           :: elenscale    ! Eddy lenght scale                        [        m]
   211)       real           :: alpha_eq10   ! Alpha (may be tweaked for convergence)   [      ---]
   212)       real           :: lam          ! Mixed term from MW99                     [      ---]
   213)       real           :: b1_mw99      ! B1 term from MW99                        [      ---]
   214)       real           :: nddfun       ! Normalised drag density function         [      ---]
   215)       real           :: ure          ! Wind speed averaged of sfc mixing length [      m/s]
   216)       real           :: sigstar      ! ustar norm. canopy velocity variance     [      ---]
   217)       real           :: sigstar3     ! Cubic of sigstar                         [      ---]
   218)       real           :: sigcomm      ! Common term for variance calculation     [      m/s]
   219)       real           :: sigma_uou2   ! Square of (sigma u / u)                  [      ---]
   220)       real           :: sigma_vou2   ! Square of (sigma v / u)                  [      ---]
   221)       real           :: sigma_wou2   ! Square of (sigma w / u)                  [      ---]
   222)       real           :: turbi        ! Mean turbulent intensity                 [      m/s]
   223)       real           :: can_reynolds ! Reynolds number of the Sfc. mixing layer [      ---]
   224)       real           :: ground_temp  ! Ground temperature                       [      ---]
   225)       real           :: stab_clm4    ! Stability parameter (CLM4, eq. 5.104)    [      ---]
   226)       logical        :: dry_grasses  ! Flag to check whether LAI+WAI is zero    [      ---]
   227)       real           :: tai_drygrass ! TAI for when a grass-only patch is dry   [    m2/m2]
   228)       real           :: c3_lad       ! c3 * lad for estimating drag coefficient [      ---]
   229)       real           :: c3_cumldrag  ! c3 * cumulative drag.                    [      ---]
   230)       real           :: snowfac_can  ! fraction of canopy covered in snow
   231)       integer        :: ibuff
   232)       !----- External functions. ----------------------------------------------------------!
   233)       real(kind=4), external :: cbrt ! Cubic root that works for negative numbers
   234)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   235) 
   236)       ibuff = 1
   237)       !$ ibuff = OMP_get_thread_num()+1
   238) 
   239)       !----- Assign some pointers. --------------------------------------------------------!
   240)       csite  => cpoly%site(isi)
   241)       cmet   => cpoly%met(isi)
   242)       cpatch => csite%patch(ipa)
   243)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   244) 
   245)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   246)       !     Find the virtual potential temperatures and decide whether the canopy air is   !
   247)       ! stable or not.                                                                     !
   248)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   249)       atm_thetav = cmet%atm_theta       * (1. + epim1 * cmet%atm_shv      )
   250)       can_thetav = csite%can_theta(ipa) * (1. + epim1 * csite%can_shv(ipa))
   251)       stable     = atm_thetav >= can_thetav
   252)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   253) 
   254) 
   255)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   256)       !     Find the free atmosphere enthalpy at the canopy air space height.              !
   257)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   258)       atm_exn_zcan  = press2exner (csite%can_prss(ipa))
   259)       atm_tmp_zcan  = extheta2temp(atm_exn_zcan,cmet%atm_theta)
   260)       atm_enthalpy  = tq2enthalpy (atm_tmp_zcan,cmet%atm_shv,.true.)
   261)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   262) 
   263) 
   264)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   265)       !     Find the enthalpy of the canopy air space.                                     !
   266)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   267)       can_enthalpy  = tq2enthalpy(csite%can_temp(ipa),csite%can_shv(ipa),.true.)
   268)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   269) 
   270) 
   271)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   272)       !     Find the specific heat at constant pressure for this canopy air space.         !
   273)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   274)       can_cp     = (1.0 - csite%can_shv(ipa)) * cpdry + csite%can_shv(ipa) * cph2o
   275)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   276) 
   277) 
   278)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   279)       !     Find the fraction of the canopy covered in snow (original snowfac function)    !
   280)       ! I think canopy roughness may need to be re-thought, but this was necessary         !
   281)       ! for turbulence & CO2 mixing to not occasionally fail sanity checks in young patches!
   282)       ! (CR)
   283)       ! Calculation of surface roughness should use the planar snow fraction, as this      !
   284)       ! should be entailing an area weighted average.  If there is instability, I argue we !
   285)       ! should address how the horizontal planar fraction is calculated, not use the       !
   286)       ! vertical as a surrogate. (RGK) I Will leave as is for now. But this needs to be    !
   287)       ! fixed.                                                                             !
   288)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   289)        snowfac_can     = min(9.9d-1,csite%total_sfcw_depth(ipa)/csite%veg_height(ipa))
   290)       !snowfac_can       = csite%snowfac(ipa)
   291)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   292) 
   293)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   294)       !     If there is no vegetation in this patch, then we apply turbulence to bare      !
   295)       ! soil, no d0 and exit.                                                              !
   296)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   297)       if (cpatch%ncohorts == 0) then
   298) 
   299)          !----- Get the appropriate characteristic wind speed. ----------------------------!
   300)          if (stable) then
   301)             cmet%vels = cmet%vels_stab
   302)          else
   303)             cmet%vels = cmet%vels_unstab
   304)          end if
   305) 
   306)          !----- Calculate the surface roughness inside the canopy. ------------------------!
   307)          csite%rough       (ipa) = soil_rough * (1.0 - snowfac_can)                        &
   308)                                  + snow_rough * snowfac_can
   309)          csite%veg_displace(ipa) = vh2dh * csite%rough(ipa) / vh2vr
   310)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   311) 
   312) 
   313)          !----- Find the characteristic scales (a.k.a. stars). ----------------------------!
   314)          call ed_stars(cmet%atm_theta,atm_enthalpy,cmet%atm_shv,cmet%atm_co2               &
   315)                       ,csite%can_theta(ipa),can_enthalpy,csite%can_shv(ipa)                &
   316)                       ,csite%can_co2(ipa),cmet%geoht,csite%veg_displace(ipa)               &
   317)                       ,cmet%atm_ustar,cmet%vels,csite%rough(ipa),csite%ustar(ipa)          &
   318)                       ,csite%tstar(ipa),estar,csite%qstar(ipa),csite%cstar(ipa)            &
   319)                       ,csite%zeta(ipa),csite%ribulk(ipa),csite%ggbare(ipa))
   320)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   321) 
   322) 
   323)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   324)          !      This is a bare ground cohort, so there is no vegetated ground.  Assign     !
   325)          ! zero to the conductance, but it shouldn't be used at all.                       !
   326)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   327)          csite%ggveg(ipa) = 0.0
   328)          csite%ggnet(ipa) = csite%ggbare(ipa)
   329)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   330) 
   331) 
   332) 
   333)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   334)          !     Calculate the heat and mass storage capacity of the canopy.                 !
   335)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   336)          call can_whccap(csite%can_rhos(ipa),csite%can_depth(ipa)                          &
   337)                         ,wcapcan,hcapcan,ccapcan,wcapcani,hcapcani,ccapcani)
   338)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   339)          return
   340)       end if
   341)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   342) 
   343) 
   344) 
   345)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   346)       !     Reset scratch variables in canopy_layer_coms.                                  !
   347)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   348)       call zero_canopy_layer('canopy_turbulence',canstr(ibuff))
   349)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   350) 
   351) 
   352) 
   353)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   354)       !     In case we do have cohorts, choose which method we use to compute the          !
   355)       ! resistance.                                                                        !
   356)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   357)       select case (icanturb)
   358) 
   359)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   360)       ! 0.  LEAF-3 Case: This approach is very similar to older implementations of ED-2,   !
   361)       !     and it is very similar to LEAF-3, and to option 1, except that option 1        !
   362)       !     computes the vegetated ground conductance and wind profile differently.        !
   363)       ! 4.  CLM-4 Case: This is the same as option 0., except that the ground conductance  !
   364)       !     is found using equations (5.99-5.104) from CLM technical note (except the bare !
   365)       !     ground, which is still found using the similarity theory).                     !
   366)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   367)       case (0,4)
   368) 
   369)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   370)          !     Find the roughness as the average between the bare ground and vegetated     !
   371)          ! ground, and apply the snow cover to further scale it.  The weighting factors    !
   372)          ! are the fraction of open canopy and the fraction of the canopy buried in snow.  !
   373)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   374)          csite%rough(ipa) = snow_rough * snowfac_can                                       &
   375)                           + ( soil_rough           * csite%opencan_frac(ipa)               &
   376)                             + csite%veg_rough(ipa) * (1.0 - csite%opencan_frac(ipa)) )     &
   377)                           * (1.0 - snowfac_can)
   378)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   379) 
   380) 
   381) 
   382)          !----- Get the appropriate characteristic wind speed. ----------------------------!
   383)          if (stable) then
   384)             cmet%vels = cmet%vels_stab
   385)          else
   386)             cmet%vels = cmet%vels_unstab
   387)          end if
   388)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   389) 
   390) 
   391) 
   392)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   393)          !      Get ustar for the ABL, assume it is a dynamic shear layer that generates a !
   394)          ! logarithmic profile of velocity.                                                !
   395)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   396)          call ed_stars(cmet%atm_theta,atm_enthalpy,cmet%atm_shv,cmet%atm_co2               &
   397)                       ,csite%can_theta(ipa),can_enthalpy,csite%can_shv(ipa)                &
   398)                       ,csite%can_co2(ipa),cmet%geoht,csite%veg_displace(ipa)               &
   399)                       ,cmet%atm_ustar,cmet%vels,csite%rough(ipa),csite%ustar(ipa)          &
   400)                       ,csite%tstar(ipa),estar,csite%qstar(ipa),csite%cstar(ipa)            &
   401)                       ,csite%zeta(ipa),csite%ribulk(ipa),csite%ggbare(ipa))
   402)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   403) 
   404) 
   405) 
   406)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   407)          !     Find the aerodynamic resistance due to vegetated ground.                    !
   408)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   409)          if (snowfac_can < 0.9) then
   410)             select case (icanturb)
   411)             case (0)
   412)                !----- LEAF-3 method. ------------------------------------------------------!
   413)                factv  = log((cmet%geoht - csite%veg_displace(ipa)) / csite%rough(ipa))     &
   414)                       / (vonk * vonk * cmet%vels)
   415)                aux    = exp(exar * (1. - (csite%veg_displace(ipa) + csite%rough(ipa))      &
   416)                                        / csite%veg_height(ipa)) )
   417)                csite%ggveg(ipa) = (exar * (csite%veg_height(ipa)-csite%veg_displace(ipa))) &
   418)                                 / (factv * csite%veg_height(ipa) * (exp(exar) - aux))
   419)             case (4)
   420)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   421)                !     Similar to CLM-4, although we use the ground conductance for bare     !
   422)                ! ground from the similarity theory and the fraction of open canopy to      !
   423)                ! determine the weights.  The conductance for dense canopy is simply        !
   424)                ! Cs_dense * u*, but Cs_dense depends on the stability.                     !
   425)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   426)                !----- Find which ground temperature to use. -------------------------------!
   427)                ksn = csite%nlev_sfcwater(ipa)
   428)                if (ksn == 0) then
   429)                   ground_temp = csite%soil_tempk(nzg,ipa)
   430)                else
   431)                   ground_temp = csite%sfcwater_tempk(ksn,ipa)
   432)                end if
   433)                !----- Stability factor (0 when it is unstable). ---------------------------!
   434)                stab_clm4        = max( 0.0                                                 &
   435)                                      , min(10., 2.0 * grav * csite%veg_height(ipa)         &
   436)                                               * ( csite%can_temp(ipa) - ground_temp )      &
   437)                                               / ( ( csite%can_temp(ipa) + ground_temp)     &
   438)                                               * csite%ustar(ipa) * csite%ustar(ipa) ) ) )
   439)                !----- The "veg" conductance is equivalent to CLM4 dense canopy. -----------!
   440)                csite%ggveg(ipa) = cs_dense0 * csite%ustar(ipa)                             &
   441)                                 / (1.0 + gamma_clm4 * stab_clm4)
   442)             end select
   443)          else
   444)             csite%ggveg(ipa) = 0.
   445)          end if
   446)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   447) 
   448) 
   449) 
   450)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   451)          !     Find the wind profile.  This is done by scaling the wind at the top of the  !
   452)          ! canopy in a method based on Leuning et al. (1995), but with some important      !
   453)          ! differences, depending on the crown model chosen.                               !
   454)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   455)          !----- Find the wind at the top of the canopy. -----------------------------------!
   456)          uh = reduced_wind(csite%ustar(ipa),csite%zeta(ipa),csite%ribulk(ipa),cmet%geoht   &
   457)                           ,csite%veg_displace(ipa),cpatch%hite(1),csite%rough(ipa))
   458)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   459)          !     In this version we still base ourselves on the Leuning et al. (1995) model, !
   460)          ! assuming each cohort to be on top of each other (i.e. no ties).  We also assume !
   461)          ! extinction to be limited to the finite crown area, so the bottom can get a bit  !
   462)          ! windier.                                                                        !
   463)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   464)          do ico=1,cpatch%ncohorts
   465)             !----- Find the extinction coefficients. --------------------------------------!
   466)             extinct_half = cpatch%crown_area(ico)                                          &
   467)                          * exp(- 0.25 * cpatch%lai(ico) / cpatch%crown_area(ico))          &
   468)                          + (1. - cpatch%crown_area(ico))
   469)             extinct_full = cpatch%crown_area(ico)                                          &
   470)                          * exp(- 0.50 * cpatch%lai(ico) / cpatch%crown_area(ico))          &
   471)                          + (1. - cpatch%crown_area(ico))
   472)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   473) 
   474)             !----- Assume that wind is at the middle of the thin crown. -------------------!
   475)             cpatch%veg_wind(ico) = max(ugbmin, uh * extinct_half)
   476)             uh                   = uh * extinct_full
   477)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   478)          end do
   479)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   480) 
   481) 
   482) 
   483) 
   484)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   485)          !   Find the aerodynamic conductances.                                            !
   486)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   487)          do ico=1,cpatch%ncohorts
   488) 
   489)             !----- Calculate the wind speed at height z. ----------------------------------!
   490)             ipft       = cpatch%pft(ico)
   491) 
   492)             if (cpatch%leaf_resolvable(ico)) then
   493)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   494)                !    Find the aerodynamic conductances for heat and water at the leaf       !
   495)                ! boundary layer.                                                           !
   496)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   497)                call leaf_aerodynamic_conductances(ipft,cpatch%veg_wind(ico)                &
   498)                                                  ,cpatch%leaf_temp(ico)                    &
   499)                                                  ,csite%can_temp(ipa)                      &
   500)                                                  ,csite%can_rhos(ipa)                      &
   501)                                                  ,can_cp                                   &
   502)                                                  ,cpatch%leaf_gbh(ico)                     &
   503)                                                  ,cpatch%leaf_gbw(ico))
   504)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   505)             else
   506)                cpatch%leaf_gbh(ico)      = 0.0
   507)                cpatch%leaf_gbw(ico)      = 0.0
   508)             end if
   509)             if (cpatch%wood_resolvable(ico)) then
   510)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   511)                !    Find the aerodynamic conductances for heat and water at the wood       !
   512)                ! boundary layer.                                                           !
   513)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   514)                call wood_aerodynamic_conductances(ipft,cpatch%dbh(ico),cpatch%hite(ico)    &
   515)                                                  ,cpatch%veg_wind(ico)                     &
   516)                                                  ,cpatch%wood_temp(ico)                    &
   517)                                                  ,csite%can_temp(ipa)                      &
   518)                                                  ,csite%can_rhos(ipa)                      &
   519)                                                  ,can_cp                                   &
   520)                                                  ,cpatch%wood_gbh(ico)                     &
   521)                                                  ,cpatch%wood_gbw(ico))
   522)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   523)             else
   524)                cpatch%wood_gbh(ico)      = 0.0
   525)                cpatch%wood_gbw(ico)      = 0.0
   526)             end if
   527)          end do
   528)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   529) 
   530) 
   531) 
   532) 
   533)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   534)          !     Calculate the heat and mass storage capacity of the canopy.                 !
   535)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   536)          call can_whccap(csite%can_rhos(ipa),csite%can_depth(ipa)                          &
   537)                         ,wcapcan,hcapcan,ccapcan,wcapcani,hcapcani,ccapcani)
   538)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   539) 
   540) 
   541)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   542)          !     Find the net ground conductance.  The net conductance is derived from the   !
   543)          ! net resistance, which is, in turn, the weighted average of the resistances in   !
   544)          ! bare and vegetated grounds.                                                     !
   545)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   546)          if (csite%opencan_frac(ipa) > 0.999 .or. snowfac_can >= 0.9) then
   547)             csite%ggnet(ipa) = csite%ggbare(ipa)
   548)          else
   549)             csite%ggnet(ipa) = csite%ggbare(ipa) * csite%ggveg(ipa)                        &
   550)                              / ( csite%ggveg(ipa) + (1. - csite%opencan_frac(ipa))         &
   551)                                                   * csite%ggbare(ipa))
   552)          end if
   553)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   554)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   555) 
   556) 
   557) 
   558) 
   559) 
   560)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   561)       ! ED-2.1 Case: There uses a slightly different approach for wind profile and ground  !
   562)       !              conductance.                                                          !
   563)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   564)       case (1)
   565)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   566)          !     The roughness is found by combining two weighted averages.  The first one   !
   567)          ! checks the fraction of the patch that has closed canopy, and averages between   !
   568)          ! soil and vegetation roughness.  The other is the fraction of the vegetation     !
   569)          ! that is covered in snow.                                                        !
   570)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   571)          csite%rough(ipa) = snow_rough * snowfac_can                                       &
   572)                           + ( soil_rough           * csite%opencan_frac(ipa)               &
   573)                             + csite%veg_rough(ipa) * (1.0 - csite%opencan_frac(ipa)) )     &
   574)                           * (1.0 - snowfac_can)
   575)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   576) 
   577) 
   578) 
   579)          !----- Calculate the soil surface roughness inside the canopy. -------------------!
   580)          surf_rough = soil_rough * (1.0 - snowfac_can)                                     &
   581)                     + snow_rough * snowfac_can
   582)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   583) 
   584) 
   585) 
   586)          !----- Get the appropriate characteristic wind speed. ----------------------------!
   587)          if (stable) then
   588)             cmet%vels = cmet%vels_stab
   589)          else
   590)             cmet%vels = cmet%vels_unstab
   591)          end if
   592)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   593) 
   594) 
   595) 
   596)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   597)          !      Get ustar for the ABL, assume it is a dynamic shear layer that generates a !
   598)          ! logarithmic profile of velocity.                                                !
   599)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   600)          call ed_stars(cmet%atm_theta,atm_enthalpy,cmet%atm_shv,cmet%atm_co2               &
   601)                       ,csite%can_theta(ipa),can_enthalpy,csite%can_shv(ipa)                &
   602)                       ,csite%can_co2(ipa),cmet%geoht,csite%veg_displace(ipa)               &
   603)                       ,cmet%atm_ustar,cmet%vels,csite%rough(ipa),csite%ustar(ipa)          &
   604)                       ,csite%tstar(ipa),estar,csite%qstar(ipa),csite%cstar(ipa)            &
   605)                       ,csite%zeta(ipa),csite%ribulk(ipa),csite%ggbare(ipa))
   606)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   607) 
   608) 
   609) 
   610) 
   611)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   612)          !     The surface resistance in the sub-canopy layer is the integration of        !
   613)          ! inverse K, from the rough soil surface, to the reference point in the canopy    !
   614)          ! where the state variable is integrated (canopy top ~ h).                        !
   615)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   616)          K_top            = vonk * csite%ustar(ipa)                                        &
   617)                           * (csite%veg_height(ipa) - csite%veg_displace(ipa))
   618)          csite%ggveg(ipa) = ( exar * K_top )                                               &
   619)                           / ( exp(exar) * csite%veg_height(ipa)                            &
   620)                             * (exp(-exar * surf_rough/csite%veg_height(ipa)) - exp(-exar)))
   621)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   622) 
   623) 
   624) 
   625)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   626)          !     This part of the code finds the geometry of the canopy air space, the       !
   627)          ! structure of the vegetation and its attenuation effects and the heat and water  !
   628)          ! capacities.                                                                     !
   629)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   630)          !----- Top of canopy wind speed. -------------------------------------------------!
   631)          uh   = reduced_wind(csite%ustar(ipa),csite%zeta(ipa),csite%ribulk(ipa),cmet%geoht &
   632)                             ,csite%veg_displace(ipa),cpatch%hite(1),csite%rough(ipa))
   633)          htop = cpatch%hite(1)
   634)          do ico=1,cpatch%ncohorts
   635) 
   636)             !----- Alias for PFT type. ----------------------------------------------------!
   637)             ipft  = cpatch%pft(ico)
   638)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   639) 
   640) 
   641) 
   642)             !----- Estimate the height at the crown midpoint. -----------------------------!
   643)             htopcrown = cpatch%hite(ico)
   644)             hbotcrown = h2crownbh(cpatch%hite(ico),ipft)
   645)             hmidcrown = 0.5 * (htopcrown + hbotcrown)
   646)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   647) 
   648) 
   649) 
   650)             !----- Calculate the wind speed at height z. ----------------------------------!
   651)             cpatch%veg_wind(ico) = max( ugbmin                                             &
   652)                                       , uh * exp(-exar * (1.0 - hmidcrown/htop)))
   653)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   654) 
   655) 
   656) 
   657)             if (cpatch%leaf_resolvable(ico)) then
   658)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   659)                !    Find the aerodynamic conductances for heat and water at the leaf       !
   660)                ! boundary layer.                                                           !
   661)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   662)                call leaf_aerodynamic_conductances(ipft,cpatch%veg_wind(ico)                &
   663)                                                  ,cpatch%leaf_temp(ico)                    &
   664)                                                  ,csite%can_temp(ipa)                      &
   665)                                                  ,csite%can_rhos(ipa)                      &
   666)                                                  ,can_cp                                   &
   667)                                                  ,cpatch%leaf_gbh(ico)                     &
   668)                                                  ,cpatch%leaf_gbw(ico))
   669)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   670)             else
   671)                cpatch%leaf_gbh(ico)      = 0.0
   672)                cpatch%leaf_gbw(ico)      = 0.0
   673)             end if
   674)             if (cpatch%wood_resolvable(ico)) then
   675)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   676)                !    Find the aerodynamic conductances for heat and water at the wood       !
   677)                ! boundary layer.                                                           !
   678)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   679)                call wood_aerodynamic_conductances(ipft,cpatch%dbh(ico),cpatch%hite(ico)    &
   680)                                                  ,cpatch%veg_wind(ico)                     &
   681)                                                  ,cpatch%wood_temp(ico)                    &
   682)                                                  ,csite%can_temp(ipa)                      &
   683)                                                  ,csite%can_rhos(ipa)                      &
   684)                                                  ,can_cp                                   &
   685)                                                  ,cpatch%wood_gbh(ico)                     &
   686)                                                  ,cpatch%wood_gbw(ico))
   687)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   688)             else
   689)                cpatch%wood_gbh(ico)      = 0.0
   690)                cpatch%wood_gbw(ico)      = 0.0
   691)             end if
   692)          end do
   693)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   694) 
   695) 
   696) 
   697)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   698)          !     Calculate the heat and mass storage capacity of the canopy.                 !
   699)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   700)          call can_whccap(csite%can_rhos(ipa),csite%can_depth(ipa)                          &
   701)                         ,wcapcan,hcapcan,ccapcan,wcapcani,hcapcani,ccapcani)
   702)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   703) 
   704) 
   705)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   706)          !     Find the net ground conductance.  The net conductance is derived from the   !
   707)          ! net resistance, which is, in turn, the weighted average of the resistances in   !
   708)          ! bare and vegetated grounds.                                                     !
   709)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   710)          if (csite%opencan_frac(ipa) > 0.999 .or. snowfac_can >= 0.9) then
   711)             csite%ggnet(ipa) = csite%ggbare(ipa)
   712)          else
   713)             csite%ggnet(ipa) = csite%ggbare(ipa) * csite%ggveg(ipa)                        &
   714)                              / ( csite%ggveg(ipa)  + (1. - csite%opencan_frac(ipa))        &
   715)                                                    * csite%ggbare(ipa) )
   716)          end if
   717)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   718)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   719) 
   720) 
   721) 
   722) 
   723)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   724)       !      Use the methods of Massman (1997) or Massman and Weil (1999).  Using option 2 !
   725)       ! will make the within-canopy drag and sheltering factors to be constant (default    !
   726)       ! Massman 1997), whereas option 3 will allow the values to vary and use the second   !
   727)       ! order closure as in Massman and Weil (1999).                                       !
   728)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   729)       case (2:3)
   730)          !----- Get the appropriate characteristic wind speed. ----------------------------!
   731)          if (stable) then
   732)             cmet%vels = cmet%vels_stab
   733)          else
   734)             cmet%vels = cmet%vels_unstab
   735)          end if
   736)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   737) 
   738) 
   739) 
   740)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   741)          !    Find the top layer and the top height.                                       !
   742)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   743)          zcan = min(ncanlyr,ceiling((cpatch%hite(1) * zztop0i)**ehgti))
   744)          htop = zztop(zcan)
   745)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   746) 
   747) 
   748) 
   749) 
   750)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   751)          !     Loop through cohorts, and integrate the leaf area density.  Notice that we  !
   752)          ! will be solving all cohorts here, because even those with no leaves have        !
   753)          ! effects on the canopy air turbulence.  We only need to decide whether we have   !
   754)          ! branches or not, and we do it outside the loop to make it more efficient.       !
   755)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   756)          !----- Reset the leaf area density array. ----------------------------------------!
   757)          canstr(ibuff)%lad(:) = 0.0
   758)          select case (ibranch_thermo)
   759)          case (0)
   760)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   761)             !     Although we are not solving branch thermodynamics, we have some drag     !
   762)             ! effect due to the branches.  Assume branch area index to be 15% of on-       !
   763)             ! -allometry LAI.  Even for grasses we may want to keep some residual due to   !
   764)             ! dead material that remains around.                                           !
   765)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   766)             do ico=1,cpatch%ncohorts
   767)                ipft = cpatch%pft(ico)
   768) 
   769) 
   770)                !------ Estimate WAI. ------------------------------------------------------!
   771)                if (is_grass(ipft) .and. igrass == 1) then
   772)                   !---- Use actual leaf mass for new grasses. -----------------------------!
   773)                   waiuse = 0.10 * cpatch%nplant(ico) * cpatch%sla(ico) * cpatch%bleaf(ico)
   774)                else
   775)                   !---- Use dbh for trees and old grasses. --------------------------------!
   776)                   waiuse = 0.10 * cpatch%nplant(ico) * cpatch%sla(ico)                     &
   777)                          * size2bl(cpatch%dbh(ico),cpatch%hite(ico),ipft)
   778)                end if
   779)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   780) 
   781) 
   782)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   783)                !     Find the heights, and compute the LAD of this cohort.                 !
   784)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   785)                htopcrown = cpatch%hite(ico)
   786)                hbotcrown = h2crownbh(cpatch%hite(ico),ipft)
   787)                ladcohort = (cpatch%lai(ico) + waiuse) / (htopcrown - hbotcrown)
   788)                kapartial = min(ncanlyr,floor  ((hbotcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti) + 1)
   789)                kafull    = min(ncanlyr,ceiling((hbotcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti) + 1)
   790)                kzpartial = min(ncanlyr,ceiling((htopcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti))
   791)                kzfull    = min(ncanlyr,floor  ((htopcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti))
   792)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   793) 
   794) 
   795)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   796)                !     Add the LAD for the full layers.                                      !
   797)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   798)                do k = kafull,kzfull
   799)                   canstr(ibuff)%lad(k) = canstr(ibuff)%lad(k) + ladcohort
   800)                end do
   801)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   802) 
   803) 
   804) 
   805)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   806)                !      Add the LAD for the partial layers.  The only special case is when   !
   807)                ! they are both the same layer, which must be done separately.              !
   808)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   809)                if (kapartial == kzpartial) then
   810)                   canstr(ibuff)%lad(kapartial) = canstr(ibuff)%lad(kapartial)              &
   811)                                  + ladcohort * (htopcrown        - hbotcrown       )       &
   812)                                              / (zztop(kapartial) - zzbot(kapartial))
   813)                else
   814)                   canstr(ibuff)%lad(kapartial) = canstr(ibuff)%lad(kapartial)              &
   815)                                  + ladcohort * (zztop(kapartial) - hbotcrown       )       &
   816)                                              / (zztop(kapartial) - zzbot(kapartial))
   817)                   canstr(ibuff)%lad(kzpartial) = canstr(ibuff)%lad(kzpartial)              &
   818)                                  + ladcohort * (htopcrown        - zzbot(kzpartial))       &
   819)                                              / (zztop(kzpartial) - zzbot(kzpartial))
   820)                end if
   821)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   822)             end do
   823) 
   824)          case default
   825) 
   826)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   827)             !     Branches are turned on.  In arid places, there is a chance that all      !
   828)             ! cohorts will be grasses with phenology status is set to 2.  This creates a   !
   829)             ! singularity, so we must check whether this is the case.                      !
   830)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   831)             dry_grasses = sum(cpatch%lai(:)+cpatch%wai(:)) == 0.0
   832)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   833) 
   834) 
   835)             !----- Use the default wood area index. ---------------------------------------!
   836)             do ico=1,cpatch%ncohorts
   837)                ipft = cpatch%pft(ico)
   838) 
   839)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   840)                !     Find the heights, and compute the LAD of this cohort.                 !
   841)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   842)                htopcrown = cpatch%hite(ico)
   843)                hbotcrown = h2crownbh(cpatch%hite(ico),ipft)
   844)                if (dry_grasses) then
   845)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   846)                   !     Dry grasses only.  Create a pseudo TAI so it won't be a            !
   847)                   ! singularity.                                                           !
   848)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   849)                   tai_drygrass = elongf_min * size2bl(cpatch%dbh(ico),cpatch%hite(ico),ipft)
   850)                   ladcohort    = tai_drygrass / (htopcrown - hbotcrown)
   851)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   852)                else
   853)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   854)                   !     At least one plant has branches or leaves, use the real stuff      !
   855)                   ! instead.                                                               !
   856)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   857)                   ladcohort = (cpatch%lai(ico) + cpatch%wai(ico)) / (htopcrown - hbotcrown)
   858)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   859)                end if
   860)                kapartial = min(ncanlyr,floor  ((hbotcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti) + 1)
   861)                kafull    = min(ncanlyr,ceiling((hbotcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti) + 1)
   862)                kzpartial = min(ncanlyr,ceiling((htopcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti))
   863)                kzfull    = min(ncanlyr,floor  ((htopcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti))
   864)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   865) 
   866) 
   867)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   868)                !     Add the LAD for the full layers.                                      !
   869)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   870)                do k = kafull,kzfull
   871)                   canstr(ibuff)%lad(k) = canstr(ibuff)%lad(k) + ladcohort
   872)                end do
   873)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   874) 
   875) 
   876) 
   877)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   878)                !      Add the LAD for the partial layers.  The only special case is when   !
   879)                ! they are both the same layer, which must be done separately.              !
   880)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   881)                if (kapartial == kzpartial) then
   882)                   canstr(ibuff)%lad(kapartial) = canstr(ibuff)%lad(kapartial)              &
   883)                                  + ladcohort * (htopcrown        - hbotcrown       )       &
   884)                                              / (zztop(kapartial) - zzbot(kapartial))
   885)                else
   886)                   canstr(ibuff)%lad(kapartial) = canstr(ibuff)%lad(kapartial)              &
   887)                                  + ladcohort * (zztop(kapartial) - hbotcrown       )       &
   888)                                              / (zztop(kapartial) - zzbot(kapartial))
   889)                   canstr(ibuff)%lad(kzpartial) = canstr(ibuff)%lad(kzpartial)              &
   890)                                  + ladcohort * (htopcrown        - zzbot(kzpartial))       &
   891)                                              / (zztop(kzpartial) - zzbot(kzpartial))
   892)                end if
   893)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   894)             end do
   895)          end select
   896)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   897) 
   898) 
   899) 
   900) 
   901)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   902)          !     In this loop we find the drag coefficient, the sheltering factor, and the   !
   903)          ! cumulative leaf drag area.  This must be done in a separate loop because we     !
   904)          ! need the full integral of the leaf area density before we determine these       !
   905)          ! variables.                                                                      !
   906)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   907)          !----- Decide whether to apply the sheltering effect or not. ---------------------!
   908)          select case (icanturb)
   909)          case (2)
   910)             !----- Drag when there are no plants. -----------------------------------------!
   911)             canstr(ibuff)%cdrag   (:) = cdrag1 + 0.5 * cdrag2
   912)             ldga_bk     = 0.0
   913)             do k = 1,zcan
   914)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   915)                !     Add the contribution of this layer to Massman's zeta (which we call   !
   916)                ! cumldrag here to not confuse with the other zeta from the similarity      !
   917)                ! theory).  We integrate in three steps so we save the value in the middle  !
   918)                ! of the layer.                                                             !
   919)                !                                                                           !
   920)                !     We use a re-fit of the original equation by Wohlfahrt and Cernusca    !
   921)                ! (2002) because it is simpler and because the coefficients listed in the   !
   922)                ! paper do not yield to the results shown in their fig. 3 (probably a typo) !
   923)                ! Their cdeff is cdrag / pshelter, here we fix pshelter = 1 and dump the    !
   924)                ! ratio to cdrag.                                                           !
   925)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   926)                c3_lad       = max(lnexp_min,min(lnexp_max,cdrag3 * canstr(ibuff)%lad(k)))
   927)                canstr(ibuff)%cdrag   (k)  = cdrag1 + cdrag2 / (1.0 + exp(c3_lad))
   928)                canstr(ibuff)%pshelter(k)  = 1.
   929)                lyrhalf      = 0.5 * canstr(ibuff)%lad(k) * canstr(ibuff)%cdrag(k) /        &
   930)                      canstr(ibuff)%pshelter(k) * dzcan(k)
   931)                canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(k)  = ldga_bk + lyrhalf
   932)                ldga_bk      = ldga_bk + 2.0 * lyrhalf
   933)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   934)             end do
   935)          case (3)
   936)             !----- Constant drag. ---------------------------------------------------------!
   937)             canstr(ibuff)%cdrag   (:) = cdrag0
   938)             ldga_bk     = 0.0
   939)             do k = 1,zcan
   940)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   941)                !     Add the contribution of this layer to Massman's zeta (which we call   !
   942)                ! cumldrag here to not confuse with the other zeta from the similarity      !
   943)                ! theory).  We integrate in three steps so we save the value in the middle  !
   944)                ! of the layer.                                                             !
   945)                !     Notice that pshelter is the inverse of the parametrisation shown in   !
   946)                ! Massman (1997) (a typo according to personal communication between Ryan   !
   947)                ! and Massman), since the shelter factor should be always >= 1.             !
   948)                ! Alpha_m97 is no longer 0.4 * h, but 5 so it becomes a constant.           !
   949)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   950)                canstr(ibuff)%pshelter(k)  = 1. + alpha_m97 * canstr(ibuff)%lad(k)
   951)                lyrhalf      = 0.5 * canstr(ibuff)%lad(k) * canstr(ibuff)%cdrag(k) /        &
   952)                      canstr(ibuff)%pshelter(k) * dzcan(k)
   953)                canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(k)  = ldga_bk + lyrhalf
   954)                ldga_bk      = ldga_bk + 2.0 * lyrhalf
   955)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   956)             end do
   957)          end select
   958)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   959) 
   960) 
   961) 
   962)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   963)          !    Find the ratio between u* and u at the top cohort, using Massman's equation  !
   964)          ! (6).                                                                            !
   965)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   966)          c3_cumldrag = min(lnexp_max,max(lnexp_min,c3_m97 * canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(zcan)))
   967)          ustarouh    = (c1_m97 - c2_m97 * exp(- c3_cumldrag))
   968)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   969) 
   970) 
   971) 
   972)          !----- NN is Massman's n, the coefficient of attenuation. ------------------------!
   973)          nn = 0.5 * canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(zcan) / (ustarouh * ustarouh)
   974)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   975) 
   976) 
   977) 
   978)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   979)          !     Find the ratio between zero-plane displacement height and height, and the   !
   980)          ! ratio between roughness and height.  The actual values will be defined based    !
   981)          ! on the patch-level averaged height, not the height of the tallest cohort,       !
   982)          ! because it may be an extremely sparse cohort that has little impact on the      !
   983)          ! drag.                                                                           !
   984)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   985)          d0ohgt = 1.0
   986)          do k=1,zcan
   987)             d0ohgt = d0ohgt - dzcan(k) / htop                                              &
   988)                             * exp(-2.0 * nn * (1.0 - canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(k) /           &
   989)                             canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(zcan)))
   990)          end do
   991)          z0ohgt = (1.0 - d0ohgt) * min(1.0, exp(- vonk / ustarouh + infunc))
   992)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
   993) 
   994) 
   995) 
   996) 
   997)          !----- Find the actual displacement height and roughness. ------------------------!
   998)          csite%veg_displace(ipa) = max( vh2dh  * veg_height_min                            &
   999)                                       , d0ohgt * csite%veg_height(ipa) )
  1000)          csite%rough       (ipa) = max( vh2vr  * veg_height_min                            &
  1001)                                       , z0ohgt * csite%veg_height(ipa) )
  1002)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1003) 
  1004) 
  1005) 
  1006)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1007)          !      Get ustar for the ABL, assume it is a dynamic shear layer that generates a !
  1008)          ! logarithmic profile of velocity.                                                !
  1009)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1010)          call ed_stars(cmet%atm_theta,atm_enthalpy,cmet%atm_shv,cmet%atm_co2               &
  1011)                       ,csite%can_theta(ipa),can_enthalpy,csite%can_shv(ipa)                &
  1012)                       ,csite%can_co2(ipa),cmet%geoht,csite%veg_displace(ipa)               &
  1013)                       ,cmet%atm_ustar,cmet%vels,csite%rough(ipa),csite%ustar(ipa)          &
  1014)                       ,csite%tstar(ipa),estar,csite%qstar(ipa),csite%cstar(ipa)            &
  1015)                       ,csite%zeta(ipa),csite%ribulk(ipa),csite%ggbare(ipa))
  1016)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1017) 
  1018) 
  1019) 
  1020) 
  1021)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1022)          !     Find the wind profile.                                                      !
  1023)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1024)          !----- Top of canopy wind speed. -------------------------------------------------!
  1025)          uh = reduced_wind(csite%ustar(ipa),csite%zeta(ipa),csite%ribulk(ipa),cmet%geoht   &
  1026)                           ,csite%veg_displace(ipa),htop,csite%rough(ipa))
  1027)          !----- Get the wind profile. -----------------------------------------------------!
  1028)          do k=1,zcan
  1029)             !----- Normalised drag density fraction and wind for this layer. --------------!
  1030)             nddfun     = 1.0 - canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(k) / canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(zcan)
  1031)             canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(k) = max(ugbmin, uh * exp(- nn * nddfun))
  1032)          end do
  1033)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1034) 
  1035) 
  1036) 
  1037)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1038)          !     Calculate the leaf level aerodynamic resistance.                            !
  1039)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1040)          do ico=1,cpatch%ncohorts
  1041)             ipft      = cpatch%pft(ico)
  1042) 
  1043)             !----- Find the crown relevant heights. ---------------------------------------!
  1044)             htopcrown = dble(cpatch%hite(ico))
  1045)             hbotcrown = dble(h2crownbh(cpatch%hite(ico),cpatch%pft(ico)))
  1046)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1047) 
  1048) 
  1049)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1050)             !     Find the layer indices for the bottom and top of the crown.              !
  1051)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1052)             kapartial = min(ncanlyr,floor  ((hbotcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti) + 1)
  1053)             kafull    = min(ncanlyr,ceiling((hbotcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti) + 1)
  1054)             kzpartial = min(ncanlyr,ceiling((htopcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti))
  1055)             kzfull    = min(ncanlyr,floor  ((htopcrown * zztop0i)**ehgti))
  1056)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1057) 
  1058) 
  1059)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1060)             !     Add the LAD for the full layers.                                         !
  1061)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1062)             if ( kapartial == kzpartial ) then
  1063)                !----- Cohort crown is in a single layer, copy the layer wind speed. -------!
  1064)                cpatch%veg_wind(ico) = canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(kapartial)
  1065)             else
  1066)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1067)                !      Cohort spans through multiple layers.  Use the average, weighted by  !
  1068)                ! the thickness of the layer.                                               !
  1069)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1070)                !----- Partial layers (bottom and top). ------------------------------------!
  1071)                cpatch%veg_wind(ico) =  &
  1072)                      canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(kapartial)*(zztop(kapartial)-hbotcrown)  &
  1073)                    + canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(kzpartial)*(htopcrown-zzbot(kzpartial))
  1074)                do k = kafull,kzfull
  1075)                   cpatch%veg_wind(ico) = cpatch%veg_wind(ico) +                            &
  1076)                         canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(k) * dzcan(k)
  1077)                end do
  1078)                !----- Divide by the total crown length to obtain the average wind. --------!
  1079)                cpatch%veg_wind(ico) = cpatch%veg_wind(ico) / (htopcrown - hbotcrown)
  1080)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1081)             end if
  1082)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1083) 
  1084) 
  1085) 
  1086) 
  1087)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1088)             if (cpatch%leaf_resolvable(ico)) then
  1089)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1090)                !    Find the aerodynamic conductances for heat and water at the leaf       !
  1091)                ! boundary layer.                                                           !
  1092)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1093)                call leaf_aerodynamic_conductances(ipft,cpatch%veg_wind(ico)                &
  1094)                                                  ,cpatch%leaf_temp(ico)                    &
  1095)                                                  ,csite%can_temp(ipa)                      &
  1096)                                                  ,csite%can_rhos(ipa)                      &
  1097)                                                  ,can_cp                                   &
  1098)                                                  ,cpatch%leaf_gbh(ico)                     &
  1099)                                                  ,cpatch%leaf_gbw(ico))
  1100)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1101)             else
  1102)                cpatch%leaf_gbh(ico)      = 0.0
  1103)                cpatch%leaf_gbw(ico)      = 0.0
  1104)             end if
  1105)             if (cpatch%wood_resolvable(ico)) then
  1106)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1107)                !    Find the aerodynamic conductances for heat and water at the wood       !
  1108)                ! boundary layer.                                                           !
  1109)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1110)                call wood_aerodynamic_conductances(ipft,cpatch%dbh(ico),cpatch%hite(ico)    &
  1111)                                                  ,cpatch%veg_wind(ico)                     &
  1112)                                                  ,cpatch%wood_temp(ico)                    &
  1113)                                                  ,csite%can_temp(ipa)                      &
  1114)                                                  ,csite%can_rhos(ipa)                      &
  1115)                                                  ,can_cp                                   &
  1116)                                                  ,cpatch%wood_gbh(ico)                     &
  1117)                                                  ,cpatch%wood_gbw(ico))
  1118)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1119)             else
  1120)                cpatch%wood_gbh(ico)      = 0.0
  1121)                cpatch%wood_gbw(ico)      = 0.0
  1122)             end if
  1123)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1124)          end do
  1125)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1126) 
  1127) 
  1128) 
  1129) 
  1130) 
  1131)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1132)          !     Decide how to solve the aerodynamic resistance based on the user's option.  !
  1133)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1134)          select case (icanturb)
  1135)          case (2)
  1136)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1137)             !     Find the bulk resistance by integrating the inverse of the diffusivity   !
  1138)             ! for each layer (e.g. Sellers et al. (1986)).  We assumed Km = sigma u, which !
  1139)             ! is the preferred approach by Sellers et al. (1986), and divide by the        !
  1140)             ! Prandtl number to convert Km to Kh.                                          !
  1141)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1142)             rasveg  = 0.0
  1143)             zetatop = csite%zeta(ipa) * ( htop       - csite%veg_displace(ipa) )           &
  1144)                                       / ( cmet%geoht - csite%veg_displace(ipa) )
  1145)             sigmakh = vonk * csite%ustar(ipa) * htop * (1.0 - d0ohgt)                      &
  1146)                     / ( tprandtl * uh * ( 1.0 - zetatop * dpsimdzeta(zetatop,stable)))
  1147)             do k=1,zcan
  1148)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1149)                !    Find the normalised drag density fraction and wind for this layer.     !
  1150)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1151)                Kdiff      = sigmakh * canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(k) + kvwake
  1152)                rasveg     = rasveg + dzcan(k) / Kdiff
  1153)             end do
  1154)             csite%ggveg(ipa) = 1.0 / rasveg
  1155)          case (3)
  1156)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1157)             !     Use MW99 second order analytical solution to sigma_w and turb intensity. !
  1158)             ! To calculate the Reynolds number, we will use the mean velocity over the     !
  1159)             ! depth of the mixing length scale (which is estimated as the roughness).      !
  1160)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1161) 
  1162)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1163)             !     Eddy length scale at the soil surface (estimates other than roughness    !
  1164)             ! are up for debate, but be warned this length scale is rather stable.         !
  1165)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1166)             elenscale = csite%rough(ipa)
  1167)             zels      = min(ncanlyr,ceiling((elenscale * zztop0i)**ehgti))
  1168)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1169) 
  1170) 
  1171) 
  1172)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1173)             !      Fill in with the wind using the similarity theory in case zels is       !
  1174)             ! greater than zcan.                                                           !
  1175)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1176)             do k=zcan+1,zels
  1177)                canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(k) = reduced_wind(csite%ustar(ipa),csite%zeta(ipa)    &
  1178)                                         ,csite%ribulk(ipa),cmet%geoht                      &
  1179)                                         ,csite%veg_displace(ipa),zzmid(k),csite%rough(ipa))
  1180)             end do
  1181)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1182) 
  1183) 
  1184)             !----- Initialise alpha, failure counters, and the logical flag. --------------!
  1185)             alpha_eq10   = alpha_mw99
  1186)             nalpha_fails = 0
  1187)             acomp        = .true.
  1188)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1189) 
  1190) 
  1191) 
  1192)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1193)             !   Go through this loop until we find a safe solution or if we give up and    !
  1194)             ! fall back to the first-order.                                                !
  1195)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1196)             afail: do
  1197) 
  1198)                lam = srthree * nu_mw99(1) / alpha_eq10
  1199) 
  1200)                !----- Arbitrary coefficient in analytical solution. -----------------------!
  1201)                b1_mw99 = - (9.0 * ustarouh)                                                &
  1202)                        / ( 2.0 * alpha_eq10 * nu_mw99(1)                                   &
  1203)                          * (2.25 - lam * lam * (csite%ustar(ipa)/uh) ** 4))
  1204) 
  1205)                ure   = 0.0
  1206)                turbi = 0.0
  1207)                do k=1,zels
  1208)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1209)                   !    Find the normalised drag density fraction and wind for this layer.  !
  1210)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1211)                   nddfun     = 1. - canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(k) / canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(zcan)
  1212) 
  1213)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1214)                   !    Integrate the wind speed.  It will be normalised outside the loop.  !
  1215)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1216)                   ure        = ure + canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(k) * dzcan(k)
  1217)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1218) 
  1219) 
  1220)                   !----- Sigstar, as in equation 10 of MW99. ------------------------------!
  1221)                   sigstar3   = &
  1222)                         nu_mw99(3) * exp( - lam * canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(zcan) * nddfun)   &
  1223)                         + b1_mw99 * ( exp( - 3.0 * nn * nddfun)                            &
  1224)                         - exp( - lam * canstr(ibuff)%cumldrag(zcan) * nddfun))
  1225)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1226) 
  1227) 
  1228)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1229)                   !      It is possible (but highly unlikely) that sigstar3 could be       !
  1230)                   ! negative.  Even though cubic roots of negative number are fine, it     !
  1231)                   ! wouldn't make sense to have sigma_u/sigma_v/sigma_w to be negative.    !
  1232)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1233)                   if (sigstar3 < 0.0) then
  1234)                      nalpha_fails = nalpha_fails + 1
  1235)                      alpha_eq10   = alpha_eq10 - 0.001
  1236)                      if (nalpha_fails == 10) then
  1237)                         !------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1238)                         !     Find the bulk resistance by integrating the inverse of the   !
  1239)                         ! diffusivity for each layer (e.g. Sellers et al. (1986)).  We     !
  1240)                         ! assumed Km = sigma u, which is the preferred approach by Sellers !
  1241)                         ! et al. (1986), and divide by the Prandtl number to convert Km to !
  1242)                         ! Kh.                                                              !
  1243)                         !------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1244)                         rasveg  = 0.0
  1245)                         zetatop = csite%zeta(ipa) * (htop       - csite%veg_displace(ipa)) &
  1246)                                                   / (cmet%geoht - csite%veg_displace(ipa))
  1247)                         sigmakh = vonk * csite%ustar(ipa) * htop * (1.0 - d0ohgt)          &
  1248)                                 / ( tprandtl * uh                                          &
  1249)                                   * ( 1.0 - zetatop * dpsimdzeta(zetatop,stable)))
  1250)                         do kk=1,zcan
  1251)                            Kdiff      = sigmakh * canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(kk) + kvwake
  1252)                            rasveg     = rasveg + dzcan(kk) / Kdiff
  1253)                         end do
  1254)                         csite%ggveg(ipa) = 1.0 / rasveg
  1255)                         exit afail
  1256)                      end if
  1257) 
  1258)                      cycle afail
  1259)                      !---------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1260)                   else
  1261)                      sigstar  = cbrt(sigstar3)
  1262)                      sigcomm  = csite%ustar(ipa) * sigstar * nu_mw99(1)
  1263)                      sigma_uou2 = (sigcomm * gamma_mw99(1) / canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(k)) ** 2
  1264)                      sigma_vou2 = (sigcomm * gamma_mw99(2) / canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(k)) ** 2
  1265)                      sigma_wou2 = (sigcomm * gamma_mw99(3) / canstr(ibuff)%windlyr(k)) ** 2
  1266)                      turbi    = turbi                                                      &
  1267)                               + sqrt(onethird * (sigma_uou2 + sigma_vou2 + sigma_wou2))    &
  1268)                               * dzcan(k)
  1269) 
  1270)                      exit afail
  1271)                   end if
  1272)                end do
  1273)             end do afail
  1274)             ure   = ure   / zztop(zels)
  1275)             turbi = turbi / zztop(zels)
  1276) 
  1277)             !------ Normalise the Reynolds number by diffusivity. -------------------------!
  1278)             can_reynolds = ure / ( th_diff0 * (1.0 + dth_diff * (csite%can_temp(ipa)-t00)))
  1279)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1280) 
  1281) 
  1282)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1283)             !     Find the aerodynamic conductance based on MW99.                          !
  1284)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1285)             csite%ggveg(ipa) = sqrt(elenscale)                                             &
  1286)                              * (1. + 2. * turbi) * tprandtl ** (-twothirds)                &
  1287)                              / sqrt(can_reynolds)                                          &
  1288)                              * uh * sqrt(ure / uh)
  1289)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1290)          end select
  1291)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1292) 
  1293) 
  1294)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1295)          !     Find the net ground conductance.  The net conductance is derived from the   !
  1296)          ! net resistance, which is, in turn, the weighted average of the resistances in   !
  1297)          ! bare and vegetated grounds.                                                     !
  1298)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1299)          if (csite%opencan_frac(ipa) > 0.999 .or. snowfac_can >= 0.9) then
  1300)             csite%ggnet(ipa) = csite%ggbare(ipa)
  1301)          else
  1302)             csite%ggnet(ipa) = csite%ggbare(ipa) * csite%ggveg(ipa)                        &
  1303)                              / ( csite%ggveg(ipa) + (1. - csite%opencan_frac(ipa))         &
  1304)                                                   * csite%ggbare(ipa))
  1305)          end if
  1306)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1307) 
  1308) 
  1309) 
  1310)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1311)          !     Calculate the heat and mass storage capacity of the canopy.                 !
  1312)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1313)          call can_whccap(csite%can_rhos(ipa),csite%can_depth(ipa)                          &
  1314)                         ,wcapcan,hcapcan,ccapcan,wcapcani,hcapcani,ccapcani)
  1315)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1316) 
  1317)       end select
  1318)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1319) 
  1320) 
  1321)       return
  1322)     end subroutine canopy_turbulence

  1333)    !     This is the double precision version of the above sub-routine.                    !
  1334)    !                                                                                       !
  1335)    !     The following subroutine calculates the turbulent transfer of scalar quantities   !
  1336)    ! using Monin Obukhov Similarity Theory under a few different flavors.  The basic       !
  1337)    ! assumptions are that the ground surface and canopy space is modeled as a rough wall   !
  1338)    ! boundary above which exists a dynamic sublayer, above which exists the free atmo-     !
  1339)    ! sphere.  We assume a logarithmic decay of wind speed over the dynamic sublayer, which !
  1340)    ! is due to the assumption of constant shear (ustar) in the dynamic sublayer.           !
  1341)    !                                                                                       !
  1342)    ! 0.  We apply the wind profile based on the similarity theory for the tallest cohort,  !
  1343)    !     with further reduction due to the presence of obstacles (trees), similar to       !
  1344)    !     Leuning et al. (1995) but considering a finite crown area.  The aerodynamic       !
  1345)    !     resistance from ground to canopy air is found in a similar way as in LEAF-3.      !
  1346)    !     Displacement height and roughness are linear functions of the vegetation height,  !
  1347)    !     where vegetation height is the weighted average of cohort heights, the weights    !
  1348)    !     being the basal area.                                                             !
  1349)    !     References:                                                                       !
  1350)    !                                                                                       !
  1351)    !     Leuning, R., F. M. Kelliher, D. G. G. de Pury, E. D. Schulze, 1995: Leaf          !
  1352)    !       nitrogen, photosynthesis, conductance and transpiration: scaling from leaves to !
  1353)    !       canopies.  Plant, Cell and Environ., 18, 1183-1200.                             !
  1354)    !                                                                                       !
  1355)    ! 1. This is similar to option 0, where displacement height and rougness are linear     !
  1356)    !    functions of mean canopy height, where mean canopy height is defined by the        !
  1357)    !    dead-biomass weighted average of canopy height.  Wind speed and diffusivity decay  !
  1358)    !    exponentially in the canopy on parameter alpha.  HOWEVER, if the windspeed at      !
  1359)    !    reference height is less than the canopy height (WHICH IS REALLY INNAPROPRIATE TO  !
  1360)    !    BEGIN WITH, BUT OH WELL), then revert to a calculation of ustar that uses no       !
  1361)    !    displacement height.  After ustar is calculated, then use displacement height in   !
  1362)    !    the calculation of the eddy length scale at the canopy top to find diffusivity at  !
  1363)    !    canopy top. THIS IS THE METHOD USED IN LEAF3 AND ED2.0.                            !
  1364)    !                                                                                       !
  1365)    ! 2. Follow the methods of Massman 1997.  The canopy top is taken as the height of the  !
  1366)    !    tallest cohort in the patch. The displacement height is determined by a center of  !
  1367)    !    pressure method. The wind speed inside the canopy decays exponentially on          !
  1368)    !    cumulative foliar drag elements, where the equation is scaled such that it solves  !
  1369)    !    for the balance of integrated canopy shear with dissipation.  To close the         !
  1370)    !    equations M97 assumes that the surface drag (U(h)/Ustar) is a function of the      !
  1371)    !    cumulative foliar drag surfaces.  With this, we can explicitly solve for roughness !
  1372)    !    length. Assumptions are made about the profile of canopy drag as a function of     !
  1373)    !    cohort attributes, please see the technical manual by RGK2009 for more info.       !
  1374)    !    Please also refer to the original paper by Massman for information regarding the   !
  1375)    !    basic principles of the closure scheme.                                            !
  1376)    !                                                                                       !
  1377)    !    Massman, W.J., 1997: An analytical one-Dimensional model of momentum transfer by   !
  1378)    !        vegetation of arbitrary structure. Boundary-Layer Meteorol., 83, 407-421.      !
  1379)    !    Wohlfahrt, G., and A. Cernusca, 2002: Momentum transfer by a mountain meadow       !
  1380)    !        canopy: a simulation analysis based on Massman's (1997) model.  Boundary-Layer !
  1381)    !        Meteorol., 103, 391-407.                                                       !
  1382)    !                                                                                       !
  1383)    ! 3.  This is related to option 2, but using a second-order clousure.                   !
  1384)    !                                                                                       !
  1385)    !    Massman, W. J., and J. C. Weil, 1999: An analytical one-dimension second-order     !
  1386)    !        closure model turbulence statistics and the Lagrangian time scale within and   !
  1387)    !        above plant canopies of arbitrary structure.  Boundary-Layer Meteorol., 91,    !
  1388)    !        81-107.                                                                        !
  1389)    !                                                                                       !
  1390)    ! 4.  This option is almost the same as option 1, except that the ground conductance is !
  1391)    !     found following the CLM technical note, equations (5.98-5.100).                   !
  1392)    !                                                                                       !
  1393)    !     Oleson, K. W., et al.; Technical description of the community land model (CLM)    !
  1394)    !        NCAR Technical Note NCAR/TN-461+STR, Boulder, CO, May 2004.                    !
  1395)    !                                                                                       !
  1396)    !     Ultimately, this routine solves for the resistance of water vapor and sensible    !
  1397)    ! heat from the soil surface to canopy air space and from the leaf surfaces to canopy   !
  1398)    ! air space.                                                                            !
  1399)    !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!

  1685)                                               / ( ( initp%can_temp + ground_temp)          &
  1686)                                               * initp%ustar * initp%ustar ) ) )

  1688)                initp%ggveg = cs_dense08 * initp%ustar / (1.d0 + gamma_clm48 * stab_clm4)

  1690)          else
  1691)             initp%ggveg = 0.d0
  1692)          end if

  1698)          ! canopy in a method based on Leuning et al. (1995), but with some important      !
  1699)          ! differences, depending on the crown model chosen.                               !
  1700)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1701)          !----- Find the wind at the top of the canopy. -----------------------------------!
  1702)          uh = reduced_wind8(initp%ustar,initp%zeta,initp%ribulk,rk4site%geoht              &
  1703)                            ,initp%veg_displace,dble(cpatch%hite(1)),initp%rough)
  1704) 
  1705)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1706)          !     In this version we still base ourselves on the Leuning et al. (1995) model, !
  1707)          ! assuming each cohort to be on top of each other (i.e. no ties).  We also assume !
  1708)          ! extinction to be limited to the finite crown area, so the bottom can get a bit  !
  1709)          ! windier.                                                                        !

  1712)             !----- Find the extinction coefficients. --------------------------------------!
  1713)             extinct_half = initp%crown_area(ico)                                           &
  1714)                          * exp(- 2.5d-1 * initp%lai(ico) / initp%crown_area(ico))          &
  1715)                          + (1.d0 - initp%crown_area(ico))
  1716)             extinct_full = initp%crown_area(ico)                                           &
  1717)                          * exp(- 5.d-1 * initp%lai(ico) / initp%crown_area(ico))           &
  1718)                          + (1.d0 - initp%crown_area(ico))
  1719)             !----- Assume that wind is at the middle of the thin crown. -------------------!

  1722)          end do

  1724) 
  1725) 
  1726) 
  1727) 
  1728)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1729)          !   Find the aerodynamic conductances.                                            !

  1732) 
  1733)             ipft  = cpatch%pft(ico)

  1783)             else

  1786)                initp%wood_nussfree(ico) = 0.d0

  1788)                initp%wood_gbh     (ico) = 0.d0
  1789)                initp%wood_gbw     (ico) = 0.d0

  1791)                cpatch%wood_gbw    (ico) = 0.0

  1794)          end do

  1801)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%wcapcan,                                           &

  1813)          if (initp%opencan_frac > 9.99d-1 .or. snowfac_can >= 9.d-1) then
  1814)             initp%ggnet = initp%ggbare
  1815)          else
  1816)             initp%ggnet = initp%ggbare * initp%ggveg                                       &
  1817)                         / ( initp%ggveg + (1.d0 - initp%opencan_frac) * initp%ggbare )

  1819)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1820)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1821) 
  1822) 
  1823) 
  1824) 
  1825) 
  1826)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1827)       ! ED-2.1 Case: There uses a slightly different approach for wind profile and ground  !

  1829)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1830)       case (1)
  1831)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1832)          !     The roughness is found by combining two weighted averages.  The first one   !

  1834)          ! soil and vegetation roughness.  The other is the fraction of the vegetation     !
  1835)          ! that is covered in snow.                                                        !

  1842)

  1844)          surf_rough = soil_rough8 * (1.d0 - snowfac_can) + snow_rough8 * snowfac_can

  1847) 
  1848) 
  1849)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!

  1853)          call ed_stars8(rk4site%atm_theta,initp%atm_enthalpy,rk4site%atm_shv             &

  1871) 
  1872)

  1875)          ! structure of the vegetation and its attenuation effects and the heat and water  !

  1883) 
  1884)             !----- Alias for PFT type. ----------------------------------------------------!
  1885)             ipft  = cpatch%pft(ico)

  1887) 
  1888) 
  1889)

  1891)             htopcrown = dble(cpatch%hite(ico))
  1892)             hbotcrown = dble(h2crownbh(cpatch%hite(ico),ipft))

  1894)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1895) 
  1896) 
  1897) 
  1898)             !----- Calculate the wind speed at height z. ----------------------------------!
  1899)             initp%veg_wind(ico) = max(ugbmin8                                              &
  1900)                                      , uh * exp(-exar8 * (1.d0 - hmidcrown/ htop)))

  1903)             if (initp%leaf_resolvable(ico)) then

  1905)                !    Find the aerodynamic conductances for heat and water at the leaf       !
  1906)                ! boundary layer.                                                           !
  1907)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1908)                call leaf_aerodynamic_conductances8(ipft,initp%veg_wind(ico)                &
  1909)                                                   ,initp%leaf_temp(ico),initp%can_temp     &
  1910)                                                   ,initp%can_rhos                          &
  1911)                                                   ,initp%can_cp                            &
  1912)                                                   ,initp%leaf_gbh(ico),initp%leaf_gbw(ico) &
  1913)                                                   ,initp%leaf_reynolds(ico)                &
  1914)                                                   ,initp%leaf_grashof(ico)                 &
  1915)                                                   ,initp%leaf_nussfree(ico)                &
  1916)                                                   ,initp%leaf_nussforc(ico) )
  1917)                cpatch%leaf_gbh(ico) = sngloff(initp%leaf_gbh(ico),tiny_offset)
  1918)                cpatch%leaf_gbw(ico) = sngloff(initp%leaf_gbw(ico),tiny_offset)
  1919)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1920)             else
  1921)                initp%leaf_reynolds(ico) = 0.d0
  1922)                initp%leaf_grashof (ico) = 0.d0
  1923)                initp%leaf_nussfree(ico) = 0.d0
  1924)                initp%leaf_nussforc(ico) = 0.d0
  1925)                initp%leaf_gbh     (ico) = 0.d0
  1926)                initp%leaf_gbw     (ico) = 0.d0
  1927)                cpatch%leaf_gbh    (ico) = 0.0
  1928)                cpatch%leaf_gbw    (ico) = 0.0
  1929)             end if

  1931)             if (initp%wood_resolvable(ico)) then
  1932)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1933)                !    Find the aerodynamic conductances for heat and water at the leaf       !
  1934)                ! boundary layer.                                                           !
  1935)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1936)                call wood_aerodynamic_conductances8(ipft,cpatch%dbh(ico),cpatch%hite(ico)   &
  1937)                                                   ,initp%veg_wind(ico)                     &
  1938)                                                   ,initp%wood_temp(ico),initp%can_temp     &
  1939)                                                   ,initp%can_rhos                          &
  1940)                                                   ,initp%can_cp                            &
  1941)                                                   ,initp%wood_gbh(ico),initp%wood_gbw(ico) &
  1942)                                                   ,initp%wood_reynolds(ico)                &
  1943)                                                   ,initp%wood_grashof(ico)                 &
  1944)                                                   ,initp%wood_nussfree(ico)                &
  1945)                                                   ,initp%wood_nussforc(ico) )
  1946)                cpatch%wood_gbh(ico) = sngloff(initp%wood_gbh(ico),tiny_offset)
  1947)                cpatch%wood_gbw(ico) = sngloff(initp%wood_gbw(ico),tiny_offset)
  1948)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1949)             else
  1950)                initp%wood_reynolds(ico) = 0.d0
  1951)                initp%wood_grashof (ico) = 0.d0
  1952)                initp%wood_nussfree(ico) = 0.d0

  1954)                initp%wood_gbh     (ico) = 0.d0
  1955)                initp%wood_gbw     (ico) = 0.d0
  1956)                cpatch%wood_gbh    (ico) = 0.0
  1957)                cpatch%wood_gbw    (ico) = 0.0
  1958)             end if

  1960)          end do

  1963)

  1966)          !     Calculate the heat and mass storage capacity of the canopy.                 !
  1967)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1968)          call can_whccap8(initp%can_rhos,initp%can_depth,                                  &
  1969)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%wcapcan,                                           &
  1970)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%hcapcan,                                           &
  1971)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%ccapcan,                                           &
  1972)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%wcapcani,                                          &
  1973)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%hcapcani,                                          &
  1974)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%ccapcani)
  1975) 
  1976)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1977) 
  1978) 
  1979)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1980)          !     Find the net ground conductance.  The net conductance is derived from the   !
  1981)          ! net resistance, which is, in turn, the weighted average of the resistances in   !
  1982)          ! bare and vegetated grounds.                                                     !
  1983)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1984)          if (initp%opencan_frac > 9.99d-1 .or. snowfac_can >= 9.d-1) then
  1985)             initp%ggnet = initp%ggbare
  1986)          else
  1987)             initp%ggnet = initp%ggbare * initp%ggveg                                       &
  1988)                         / (initp%ggveg + (1.d0 - initp%opencan_frac) * initp%ggbare )
  1989)          end if
  1990)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1991)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1992) 
  1993) 
  1994) 
  1995) 
  1996)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  1997)       !      Use the methods of Massman (1997) or Massman and Weil (1999).  Using option 2 !
  1998)       ! will make the within-canopy drag and sheltering factors to be constant (default    !
  1999)       ! Massman 1997), whereas option 3 will allow the values to vary and use the second   !

  2001)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2002)       case (2:3)
  2003)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2004)          !    Find the top layer and the top height.                                       !
  2005)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2006)          zcan = min(ncanlyr,ceiling((dble(cpatch%hite(1)) * zztop0i8)**ehgti8))
  2007)          htop = zztop8(zcan)
  2008)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2009) 
  2010) 
  2011)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2012)          !     Loop through cohorts, and integrate the leaf area density.  Notice that we  !

  2014)          ! effects on the canopy air turbulence.  We only need to decide whether we have   !
  2015)          ! branches or not, and we do it outside the loop to make it more efficient.       !
  2016)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2017)          !----- Reset the leaf area density array. ----------------------------------------!
  2018)          canstr(ibuff)%lad8(:) = 0.d0
  2019)          select case (ibranch_thermo)
  2020)          case (0)
  2021)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2022)             !     Although we are not solving branch thermodynamics, we have some drag     !
  2023)             ! effect due to the branches.  Assume branch area index to be 15% of on-       !
  2024)             ! -allometry LAI.  Even for grasses we may want to keep some residual due to   !

  2027)             do ico=1,cpatch%ncohorts

  2071)                if (kapartial == kzpartial) then

  2076)                   canstr(ibuff)%lad8(kapartial) = canstr(ibuff)%lad8(kapartial)            &
  2077)                                   + ladcohort * (zztop8(kapartial) - hbotcrown        )    &
  2078)                                               / (zztop8(kapartial) - zzbot8(kapartial))

  2081)                                               / (zztop8(kzpartial) - zzbot8(kzpartial))

  2083)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2084)             end do
  2085) 
  2086)          case default
  2087) 
  2088)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2089)             !     Branches are turned on.  In arid places, there is a chance that all      !
  2090)             ! cohorts will be grasses with phenology status is set to 2.  This creates a   !
  2091)             ! singularity, so we must check whether this is the case.                      !
  2092)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2093)             dry_grasses = sum(cpatch%lai(:)+cpatch%wai(:)) == 0.0
  2094)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2095) 
  2096) 
  2097)             !----- Use the default wood area index. ---------------------------------------!
  2098)             do ico=1,cpatch%ncohorts
  2099)                ipft = cpatch%pft(ico)
  2100) 
  2101)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2102)                !     Find the heights, and compute the LAD of this cohort.                 !
  2103)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2104)                htopcrown = dble(cpatch%hite(ico))
  2105)                hbotcrown = dble(h2crownbh(cpatch%hite(ico),ipft))

  2107)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!

  2110)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2111)                   tai_drygrass = dble( elongf_min                                          &
  2112)                                      * size2bl(cpatch%dbh(ico),cpatch%hite(ico),ipft))
  2113)                   ladcohort    = tai_drygrass / (htopcrown - hbotcrown)
  2114)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2115)                else
  2116)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2117)                   !     At least one plant has branches or leaves, use the real stuff      !
  2118)                   ! instead.                                                               !
  2119)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2120)                   ladcohort = (initp%lai(ico) + initp%wai(ico)) / (htopcrown - hbotcrown)
  2121)                   !------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2122)                end if
  2123)                kapartial = min(ncanlyr,floor  ((hbotcrown * zztop0i8)**ehgti8) + 1)
  2124)                kafull    = min(ncanlyr,ceiling((hbotcrown * zztop0i8)**ehgti8) + 1)
  2125)                kzpartial = min(ncanlyr,ceiling((htopcrown * zztop0i8)**ehgti8))
  2126)                kzfull    = min(ncanlyr,floor  ((htopcrown * zztop0i8)**ehgti8))
  2127)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2128) 
  2129) 
  2130)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2131)                !     Add the LAD for the full layers.                                      !
  2132)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2133)                do k = kafull,kzfull
  2134)                   canstr(ibuff)%lad8(k) = canstr(ibuff)%lad8(k) + ladcohort

  2161)

  2238) 
  2239)

  2258)

  2320)                !----- Cohort crown is in a single layer, copy the layer wind speed. -------!
  2321)                initp%veg_wind(ico) = canstr(ibuff)%windlyr8(kapartial)

  2333)                         canstr(ibuff)%windlyr8(k) * dzcan8(k)

  2411)          case (2)

  2537)                   else

  2542)                      sigma_vou2 = (sigcomm * gamma_mw99_8(2) /                             &

  2550)                   end if
  2551)                end do
  2552)             end do afail
  2553)             ure   = ure   / zztop8(zels)
  2554)             turbi = turbi / zztop8(zels)
  2555) 
  2556)             !------ Normalise the Reynolds number by diffusivity. -------------------------!
  2557)             can_reynolds = ure / ( th_diff08 * (1.d0 + dth_diff8 * (initp%can_temp-t008)))
  2558) 
  2559) 
  2560)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2561)             !     Find the aerodynamic conductance based on MW99.                          !
  2562)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2563)             initp%ggveg = sqrt(elenscale)                                                  &
  2564)                         * (1.d0 + 2.d0 * turbi) * tprandtl8 ** (-twothirds8)               &
  2565)                         / sqrt(can_reynolds)                                               &
  2566)                         * uh * sqrt(ure / uh)
  2567)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2568)          end select
  2569)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2570) 
  2571) 
  2572) 
  2573) 
  2574) 
  2575)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2576)          !     Find the net ground conductance.  The net conductance is derived from the   !
  2577)          ! net resistance, which is, in turn, the weighted average of the resistances in   !
  2578)          ! bare and vegetated grounds.                                                     !
  2579)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2580)          if (initp%opencan_frac > 9.99d-1 .or. snowfac_can >= 9.d-1) then
  2581)             initp%ggnet = initp%ggbare
  2582)          else
  2583)             initp%ggnet = initp%ggbare * initp%ggveg                                       &
  2584)                         / (initp%ggveg + (1.d0 - initp%opencan_frac) * initp%ggbare )
  2585)          end if
  2586)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2587) 
  2588) 
  2589) 
  2590)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2591)          !     Calculate the heat and mass storage capacity of the canopy.                 !
  2592)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2593)          call can_whccap8(initp%can_rhos,initp%can_depth,                                  &
  2594)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%wcapcan,                                           &
  2595)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%hcapcan,                                           &
  2596)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%ccapcan,                                           &
  2597)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%wcapcani,                                          &
  2598)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%hcapcani,                                          &
  2599)                           rk4aux(ibuff)%ccapcani)
  2600)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2601) 
  2602)       end select
  2603)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2604) 
  2605)       return
  2606)    end subroutine canopy_turbulence8

  2617)    !    This subroutine computes the characteristic scales based on surface layer          !
  2618)    ! parameterization.  Assume that stability is calculated on the potential  gradient     !
  2619)    ! from the surface to the atmosphere at reference height.  Apply the instability        !
  2620)    ! parameters to calculate the friction velocity.  Use the instability parameters for    !
  2621)    ! momentum and scalars, that were calculated over the distance from surface to refer-   !
  2622)    ! ence height to determine the heat, moisture and carbon flux rates at the canopy to    !
  2623)    ! atmosphere at reference height.                                                       !
  2624)    !    Two models are available, and the user can choose between them by setting the      !
  2625)    ! variable ISFCLYRM in ED2IN (if running the coupled model, this is done in ISTAR).     !
  2626)    !                                                                                       !
  2627)    ! 1. Based on L79;                                                                      !
  2628)    ! 2. Based on: OD95, but with some terms computed as in L79 and B71 to avoid singular-  !
  2629)    !    ities (now using the iterative method to find zeta).                               !
  2630)    ! 3. Based on BH91, using an iterative method to find zeta, and using the modified      !
  2631)    !    equation for stable layers.                                                        !
  2632)    ! 4. Based on CLM04, with special functions for very stable and very stable case, even  !
  2633)    !    though we use a different functional form for very unstable case for momentum.     !
  2634)    !    This is ensure that phi_m decreases monotonically as zeta becomes more negative.   !
  2635)    !    We use a power law of order of -1/6 instead.                                       !
  2636)    !                                                                                       !
  2637)    ! References:                                                                           !
  2638)    ! B71.  BUSINGER, J.A, et. al; Flux-Profile relationships in the atmospheric surface    !
  2639)    !           layer. J. Atmos. Sci., 28, 181-189, 1971.                                   !
  2640)    ! L79.  LOUIS, J.F.; Parametric Model of vertical eddy fluxes in the atmosphere.        !
  2641)    !           Boundary-Layer Meteor., 17, 187-202, 1979.                                  !
  2642)    ! BH91. BELJAARS, A.C.M.; HOLTSLAG, A.A.M.; Flux parameterization over land surfaces    !
  2643)    !           for atmospheric models. J. Appl. Meteor., 30, 327-341, 1991.                !
  2644)    ! OD95. ONCLEY, S.P.; DUDHIA, J.; Evaluation of surface fluxes from MM5 using observa-  !
  2645)    !           tions.  Mon. Wea. Rev., 123, 3344-3357, 1995.                               !
  2646)    ! CLM04. OLESON, K. W., et al.; Technical description of the community land model (CLM) !
  2647)    !           NCAR Technical Note NCAR/TN-461+STR, Boulder, CO, May 2004.                 !
  2648)    !                                                                                       !

  2650)    subroutine ed_stars(theta_atm,enthalpy_atm,shv_atm,co2_atm,theta_can,enthalpy_can       &
  2651)                       ,shv_can,co2_can,zref,dheight,atm_ustar,uref,rough,ustar,tstar,estar &
  2652)                       ,qstar,cstar,zeta,rib,ggbare)
  2653)       use consts_coms     , only : grav            & ! intent(in)
  2654)                                  , vonk            & ! intent(in)
  2655)                                  , epim1           & ! intent(in)
  2656)                                  , halfpi          ! ! intent(in)
  2657)       use canopy_air_coms , only : isfclyrm        & ! intent(in)
  2658)                                  , ustmin          & ! intent(in)
  2659)                                  , bl79            & ! intent(in)
  2660)                                  , csm             & ! intent(in)
  2661)                                  , csh             & ! intent(in)
  2662)                                  , dl79            & ! intent(in)
  2663)                                  , ribmax          & ! intent(in)
  2664)                                  , tprandtl        & ! intent(in)
  2665)                                  , z0moz0h         & ! intent(in)
  2666)                                  , z0hoz0m         & ! intent(in)
  2667)                                  , psim            & ! function
  2668)                                  , psih            & ! function
  2669)                                  , zoobukhov       & ! function
  2670)                                  , zoobukhov_ustar ! ! function
  2671)       use rk4_coms        , only : rk4_tolerance   ! ! intent(in)
  2672)       implicit none
  2673)       !----- Arguments. -------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2674)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: theta_atm    ! Above canopy air pot. temp.   [        K]
  2675)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: enthalpy_atm ! Above can. air spec. enthalpy [ J/kg_air]
  2676)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: shv_atm      ! Above can. vapour spec. hum.  [kg/kg_air]
  2677)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: co2_atm      ! CO2 mixing ratio              [ �mol/mol]
  2678)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: theta_can    ! Canopy air pot. temperature   [        K]
  2679)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: enthalpy_can ! Canopy air specific enthalpy  [ J/kg_air]
  2680)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: shv_can      ! Canopy air vapour spec. hum.  [kg/kg_air]
  2681)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: co2_can      ! Canopy air CO2 mixing ratio   [ �mol/mol]
  2682)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: zref         ! Height at reference point     [        m]
  2683)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: dheight      ! Zero-plane displacement hgt.  [        m]
  2684)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: atm_ustar    ! prescribed u*                 [      m/s]
  2685)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: uref         ! Wind speed at reference hgt.  [      m/s]
  2686)       real(kind=4), intent(in)  :: rough        ! Roughness                     [        m]
  2687)       real(kind=4), intent(out) :: ustar        ! U*, friction velocity         [      m/s]
  2688)       real(kind=4), intent(out) :: qstar        ! Specific humidity turb. scale [kg/kg_air]
  2689)       real(kind=4), intent(out) :: tstar        ! Temperature turbulence scale  [        K]
  2690)       real(kind=4), intent(out) :: estar        ! Spec. enthalpy turb. scale    [ J/kg_air]
  2691)       real(kind=4), intent(out) :: cstar        ! CO2 mixing ratio turb. scale  [ �mol/mol]
  2692)       real(kind=4), intent(out) :: zeta         ! z/(Obukhov length).           [    -----]
  2693)       real(kind=4), intent(out) :: rib          ! Bulk richardson number.       [    -----]
  2694)       real(kind=4), intent(out) :: ggbare       ! Ground conductance            [      m/s]
  2695)       !----- Local variables. -------------------------------------------------------------!
  2696)       logical                   :: stable       ! Stable state                  [      T|F]
  2697)       real(kind=4)              :: zoz0m        ! zref/rough(momentum)          [    -----]
  2698)       real(kind=4)              :: lnzoz0m      ! ln[zref/rough(momentum)]      [    -----]
  2699)       real(kind=4)              :: zoz0h        ! zref/rough(heat)              [    -----]
  2700)       real(kind=4)              :: lnzoz0h      ! ln[zref/rough(heat)]          [    -----]
  2701)       real(kind=4)              :: c3           ! aux. coefficient              [    -----]
  2702)       real(kind=4)              :: uuse         ! Wind for when (Rib > Ribmax)  [      m/s]
  2703)       real(kind=4)              :: kuoustar     ! k * u / u*
  2704)       !----- Local variables, used by L79. ------------------------------------------------!
  2705)       real(kind=4)              :: a2           ! Drag coefficient in neutral conditions
  2706)       real(kind=4)              :: c1           ! a2 * vels
  2707)       real(kind=4)              :: fm           ! Stability parameter for momentum
  2708)       real(kind=4)              :: fh           ! Stability parameter for heat
  2709)       real(kind=4)              :: c2           ! Part of the c coefficient common
  2710)                                                 !      to momentum & heat.
  2711)       real(kind=4)              :: cm           ! c times |Rib|^1/2 for momentum.
  2712)       real(kind=4)              :: ch           ! c times |Rib|^1/2 for heat.
  2713)       real(kind=4)              :: ee           ! (z/z0)^1/3 -1. for eqn. 20
  2714)       !----- Local variables, used by other schemes. --------------------------------------!
  2715)       real(kind=4)              :: zstar        ! height above displacement
  2716)       real(kind=4)              :: zeta0m       ! roughness(momentum)/(Obukhov length).
  2717)       real(kind=4)              :: zeta0h       ! roughness(heat)/(Obukhov length).
  2718)       real(kind=4)              :: utotal       ! Total wind (actual + convective)
  2719)       real(kind=4)              :: uconv        ! Convective velocity
  2720)       real(kind=4)              :: uconv_prev   ! Previous convective velocity
  2721)       real(kind=4)              :: change       ! Difference in convective velocity
  2722)       integer           :: icnt         ! Iteration counter
  2723)       !----- Aux. environment conditions. -------------------------------------------------!
  2724)       real(kind=4)              :: thetav_atm   ! Atmos. virtual pot. temp.     [        K]
  2725)       real(kind=4)              :: thetav_can   ! Canopy air virtual pot. temp. [        K]
  2726)       !----- External functions. ----------------------------------------------------------!
  2727)       real(kind=4), external    :: cbrt         ! Cubic root
  2728)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2729) 
  2730) 
  2731) 
  2732)       !----- Find the variables common to both methods. -----------------------------------!
  2733)       thetav_atm = theta_atm * (1. + epim1 * shv_atm)
  2734)       thetav_can = theta_can * (1. + epim1 * shv_can)
  2735)       zstar      = zref-dheight
  2736)       zoz0m      = zstar / rough
  2737)       lnzoz0m    = log(zoz0m)
  2738)       zoz0h      = z0moz0h * zoz0m
  2739)       lnzoz0h    = log(zoz0h)
  2740)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2741) 
  2742) 
  2743) 
  2744)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2745)       !     Find the Bulk Richardson number.  For stable cases, and for L79 in both cases, !
  2746)       ! this will be the definitive RiB, whilst this is the first guess, which will be     !
  2747)       ! corrected by the convective velocity in the other unstable cases.                  !
  2748)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2749)       rib        = 2.0 * grav * (zstar-rough) * (thetav_atm-thetav_can)                    &
  2750)                  / ( (thetav_atm+thetav_can) * uref * uref)
  2751)       stable     = thetav_atm >= thetav_can
  2752)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2753) 
  2754) 
  2755) 
  2756) 
  2757)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2758)       !    Correct the bulk Richardson number in case it's too stable.  We also define a   !
  2759)       ! stable case correction to make u* consistent with the Richardson number.           !
  2760)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2761)       if (rib > ribmax) then
  2762)          uuse = sqrt(rib / ribmax) * uref
  2763)          rib  = ribmax
  2764)       else
  2765)          uuse = uref
  2766)       end if
  2767)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2768) 
  2769) 
  2770) 
  2771) 
  2772)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2773)       !     Here we find u* and the coefficient to find the other stars based on the       !
  2774)       ! chosen surface model.                                                              !
  2775)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2776)       select case (isfclyrm)
  2777)       case (0)
  2778)          !----- Use prescribed u* instead of calculating it. ------------------------------!
  2779)          ustar    = max(ustmin,atm_ustar)
  2780)          kuoustar = vonk * uref / ustar
  2781)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2782) 
  2783) 
  2784)          !----- Find the dimensionless height. --------------------------------------------!
  2785)          zeta   = zoobukhov_ustar(rib,zstar,rough,zoz0h,lnzoz0h,kuoustar,stable)
  2786)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2787) 
  2788) 
  2789)          !----- Find the dimensionless roughness. -----------------------------------------!
  2790)          zeta0m = rough * zeta / zstar
  2791)          zeta0h = z0hoz0m * zeta0m
  2792)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2793) 
  2794) 
  2795)          !----- Find the coefficient to scale the other stars. ----------------------------!
  2796)          c3    = vonk / (tprandtl * (lnzoz0h - psih(zeta,stable) + psih(zeta0h,stable)))
  2797)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2798) 
  2799)       case (1)
  2800) 
  2801)          !----- Compute the a-square factor and the coefficient to find theta*. -----------!
  2802)          a2   = vonk * vonk / (lnzoz0m * lnzoz0m)
  2803)          c1   = a2 * uuse
  2804) 
  2805)          if (stable) then
  2806)             !----- Stable case ------------------------------------------------------------!
  2807) 
  2808)             fm = 1.0 / (1.0 + (2.0 * bl79 * rib / sqrt(1.0 + dl79 * rib)))
  2809)             fh = 1.0 / (1.0 + (3.0 * bl79 * rib * sqrt(1.0 + dl79 * rib)))
  2810) 
  2811)          else
  2812) 
  2813)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2814)             !     Unstable case.  The only difference from the original method is that we  !
  2815)             ! no longer assume z >> z0, so the "c" coefficient uses the full z/z0 term.    !
  2816)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2817)             ee = cbrt(zoz0m) - 1.
  2818)             c2 = bl79 * a2 * ee * sqrt(ee * abs(rib))
  2819)             cm = csm * c2
  2820)             ch = csh * c2
  2821)             fm = (1.0 - 2.0 * bl79 * rib / (1.0 + 2.0 * cm))
  2822)             fh = (1.0 - 3.0 * bl79 * rib / (1.0 + 3.0 * ch))
  2823)          end if
  2824) 
  2825)          !----- Finding ustar, making sure it is not too small. ---------------------------!
  2826)          ustar = max(ustmin,sqrt(c1 * uuse * fm))
  2827)          !----- Finding the coefficient to scale the other stars. -------------------------!
  2828)          c3 = c1 * fh / ustar
  2829) 
  2830)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2831) 
  2832)          !----- Compute zeta from u* and T* -----------------------------------------------!
  2833)          zeta = grav * vonk * c3 * (thetav_atm - thetav_can) / (thetav_atm * ustar * ustar)
  2834) 
  2835) 
  2836)       case default
  2837)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2838)          ! 2. Here we use the model proposed by OD95, the standard for MM5, but with some  !
  2839)          !    terms that were computed in B71 (namely, the "0" terms), which prevent sin-  !
  2840)          !    gularities.                                                                  !
  2841)          !    However we know zeta, so zeta0 can be written as z0/z * zeta.                !
  2842)          ! 3. Here we use the model proposed by BH91, which is almost the same as the OD95 !
  2843)          !    method, except that the stable functions are computed in a more generic way. !
  2844)          !    BH91 claim that the oft-used approximation (-beta*zeta) can cause poor       !
  2845)          !    ventilation of the stable layer, leading to decoupling between the atmo-     !
  2846)          !    sphere and the canopy air space and excessive cooling                        !
  2847)          ! 4. Here we use a similar approach as in CLM04, excepth that the momentum flux   !
  2848)          !    gradient function for the unstable case for momentum is switched by a power  !
  2849)          !    of -1/6 (kind of the square of the heat one).  This is to guarantee that     !
  2850)          !    the psi function doesn't have local maxima/minima.                           !
  2851)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2852)          !----- Initialise uconv. ---------------------------------------------------------!
  2853)          uconv      = 0.0
  2854)          !----- Check if we need to go through the iterative process. ---------------------!
  2855)          if (stable) then
  2856)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2857)             !     Stable case, we don't need to find convective velocity, so we don't need !
  2858)             ! the iterative case.                                                          !
  2859)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2860)             !----- Find the dimensionless height. -----------------------------------------!
  2861)             zeta   = zoobukhov(rib,zstar,rough,zoz0m,lnzoz0m,zoz0h,lnzoz0h,stable)
  2862)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2863) 
  2864) 
  2865)             !----- Find the dimensionless roughness. --------------------------------------!
  2866)             zeta0m = rough * zeta / zstar
  2867)             zeta0h = z0hoz0m * zeta0m
  2868)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2869) 
  2870) 
  2871)             !----- Find ustar, making sure it is not too small. ---------------------------!
  2872)             ustar = max (ustmin, vonk * uuse                                               &
  2873)                                      / (lnzoz0m - psim(zeta,stable) + psim(zeta0m,stable)))
  2874)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2875) 
  2876) 
  2877)             !----- Find the coefficient to scale the other stars. -------------------------!
  2878)             c3    = vonk / (tprandtl * (lnzoz0h - psih(zeta,stable) + psih(zeta0h,stable)))
  2879)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2880) 
  2881)          else
  2882)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2883)             !    Unstable case.  Here we run a few iterations to make sure we correct the  !
  2884)             ! bulk Richardson number.  This is really a simple correction, so we don't     !
  2885)             ! need uconv to be totally in equilibrium.                                     !
  2886)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2887)             unstable: do icnt=1,6
  2888)                !----- Update total winds. -------------------------------------------------!
  2889)                uconv_prev = uconv
  2890)                utotal     = sqrt(uuse*uuse + uconv_prev * uconv_prev)
  2891)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2892) 
  2893) 
  2894)                !----- Update the Bulk Richardson number. ----------------------------------!
  2895)                rib        = 2.0 * grav * (zstar-rough) * (thetav_atm-thetav_can)           &
  2896)                           / ( (thetav_atm+thetav_can) * utotal)
  2897)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2898) 
  2899) 
  2900)                !----- Find the dimensionless height. --------------------------------------!
  2901)                zeta   = zoobukhov(rib,zstar,rough,zoz0m,lnzoz0m,zoz0h,lnzoz0h,stable)
  2902)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2903) 
  2904) 
  2905)                !----- Find the dimensionless roughness. -----------------------------------!
  2906)                zeta0m = rough * zeta / zstar
  2907)                zeta0h = z0hoz0m * zeta0m
  2908)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2909) 
  2910) 
  2911)                !----- Find ustar, making sure it is not too small. ------------------------!
  2912)                ustar = max (ustmin, vonk * uuse                                            &
  2913)                                   / (lnzoz0m - psim(zeta,stable) + psim(zeta0m,stable)))
  2914)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2915) 
  2916) 
  2917)                !----- Find the coefficient to scale the other stars. ----------------------!
  2918)                c3    = vonk                                                                &
  2919)                      / (tprandtl * (lnzoz0h - psih(zeta,stable) + psih(zeta0h,stable)))
  2920)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2921) 
  2922) 
  2923)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2924)                !     Use potential virtual temperature here because convection is related  !
  2925)                ! to buoyancy.                                                              !
  2926)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2927)                tstar  = c3 * (thetav_atm - thetav_can )
  2928)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2929) 
  2930) 
  2931)                !----- Estimate the convective velocity. -----------------------------------!
  2932)                uconv = vertical_vel_flux(zeta,ustar) / ustar
  2933)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2934) 
  2935) 
  2936)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2937)                !     We are only after a rough estimate of this velocity, so if the        !
  2938)                ! difference is less than the RK4 tolerance, then it's enough.              !
  2939)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2940)                change = 2.0 * abs(uconv-uconv_prev) / (abs(uconv) + abs(uconv_prev))
  2941)                if (change < rk4_tolerance) exit unstable
  2942)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2943)             end do unstable
  2944)          end if
  2945)       end select
  2946) 
  2947)       !----- Compute the other scales. ----------------------------------------------------!
  2948)       qstar = c3 * (shv_atm      - shv_can      )
  2949)       tstar = c3 * (theta_atm    - theta_can    )
  2950)       estar = c3 * (enthalpy_atm - enthalpy_can )
  2951)       cstar = c3 * (co2_atm      - co2_can      )
  2952)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2953) 
  2954) 
  2955) 
  2956)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2957)       !    Compute the bare ground conductance.  This equation is similar to the original, !
  2958)       ! except that we don't assume the ratio between the gradient and the characteristic  !
  2959)       ! scale to be 0.2; instead we use the actual ratio that is computed here.            !
  2960)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2961)       ggbare  = c3 * ustar
  2962)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  2963) 
  2964)       return
  2965)    end subroutine ed_stars

  2977)    ! computes the characteristic scales based on surface layer parameterization.  Assume   !
  2978)    ! that stability is calculated on the potential gradient from the surface to the        !
  2979)    ! atmosphere at reference height.  Apply the instability parameters to calculate the    !
  2980)    ! friction velocity.  Use the instability parameters for momentum and scalars, that     !

  2982)    ! heat, moisture and carbon flux rates at the canopy to atmosphere at reference height. !

  2985)    !                                                                                       !
  2986)    ! 1. Based on L79;                                                                      !
  2987)    ! 2. Based on: OD95, but with some terms computed as in L79 and B71 to avoid singular-  !
  2988)    !    ities (now using the iterative method to find zeta).                               !
  2989)    ! 3. Based on BH91, using an iterative method to find zeta, and using the modified      !
  2990)    !    equation for stable layers.                                                        !
  2991)    ! 4. Based on CLM04, with special functions for very stable and very stable case, even  !
  2992)    !    though we use a different functional form for very unstable case for momentum.     !
  2993)    !    This is ensure that phi_m decreases monotonically as zeta becomes more negative.   !
  2994)    !    We use a power law of order of -1/6 instead.                                       !
  2995)    !                                                                                       !

  2997)    ! B71.  BUSINGER, J.A, et. al; Flux-Profile relationships in the atmospheric surface    !
  2998)    !           layer. J. Atmos. Sci., 28, 181-189, 1971.                                   !
  2999)    ! L79.  LOUIS, J.F.; Parametric Model of vertical eddy fluxes in the atmosphere.        !
  3000)    !           Boundary-Layer Meteor., 17, 187-202, 1979.                                  !
  3001)    ! BH91. BELJAARS, A.C.M.; HOLTSLAG, A.A.M.; Flux parameterization over land surfaces    !
  3002)    !           for atmospheric models. J. Appl. Meteor., 30, 327-341, 1991.                !
  3003)    ! OD95. ONCLEY, S.P.; DUDHIA, J.; Evaluation of surface fluxes from MM5 using observa-  !
  3004)    !           tions.  Mon. Wea. Rev., 123, 3344-3357, 1995.                               !
  3005)    ! CLM04. OLESON, K. W., et al.; Technical description of the community land model (CLM) !
  3006)    !           NCAR Technical Note NCAR/TN-461+STR, Boulder, CO, May 2004.                 !

  3020)                                  , csm8             & ! intent(in)

  3042)       real(kind=8), intent(in)  :: zref         ! Height at reference point     [        m]
  3043)       real(kind=8), intent(in)  :: dheight      ! Zero-plane displacement hgt.  [        m]
  3044)       real(kind=8), intent(in)  :: atm_ustar    ! Prescribed u*                 [      m/s]
  3045)       real(kind=8), intent(in)  :: uref         ! Wind speed at reference hgt.  [      m/s]
  3046)       real(kind=8), intent(in)  :: rough        ! Roughness                     [        m]

  3049)       real(kind=8), intent(out) :: tstar        ! Temperature turbulence scale  [        K]
  3050)       real(kind=8), intent(out) :: estar        ! Spec. enthalpy turb. scale    [ J/kg_air]
  3051)       real(kind=8), intent(out) :: cstar        ! CO2 mixing ratio turb. scale  [ �mol/mol]
  3052)       real(kind=8), intent(out) :: zeta         ! z/(Obukhov length).           [    -----]
  3053)       real(kind=8), intent(out) :: rib          ! Bulk richardson number.       [    -----]
  3054)       real(kind=8), intent(out) :: ggbare       ! Ground conductance            [      m/s]
  3055)       !----- Local variables --------------------------------------------------------------!
  3056)       logical                   :: stable       ! Stable state                  [      T|F]
  3057)       real(kind=8)              :: zoz0m        ! zref/rough(momentum)          [    -----]
  3058)       real(kind=8)              :: lnzoz0m      ! ln[zref/rough(momentum)]      [    -----]
  3059)       real(kind=8)              :: zoz0h        ! zref/rough(heat)              [    -----]
  3060)       real(kind=8)              :: lnzoz0h      ! ln[zref/rough(heat)]          [    -----]
  3061)       real(kind=8)              :: c3           ! aux. coefficient              [    -----]
  3062)       real(kind=8)              :: uuse         ! Wind for when (Rib > Ribmax)  [      m/s]
  3063)       real(kind=8)              :: kuoustar     ! k * u / u*
  3064)       !----- Local variables, used by L79. ------------------------------------------------!
  3065)       real(kind=8)              :: a2           ! Drag coefficient in neutral conditions
  3066)       real(kind=8)              :: c1           ! a2 * vels
  3067)       real(kind=8)              :: fm           ! Stability parameter for momentum
  3068)       real(kind=8)              :: fh           ! Stability parameter for heat
  3069)       real(kind=8)              :: c2           ! Part of the c coefficient common
  3070)                                                 !      to momentum & heat.
  3071)       real(kind=8)              :: cm           ! c times |Rib|^1/2 for momentum.
  3072)       real(kind=8)              :: ch           ! c times |Rib|^1/2 for heat.
  3073)       real(kind=8)              :: ee           ! (z/z0)^1/3 -1. for eqn. 20
  3074)       !----- Local variables, used by others. ---------------------------------------------!
  3075)       real(kind=8)              :: zstar        ! Reference height above displacement
  3076)       real(kind=8)              :: zeta0m       ! roughness(momentum)/(Obukhov length).
  3077)       real(kind=8)              :: zeta0h       ! roughness(heat)/(Obukhov length).
  3078)       real(kind=8)              :: utotal       ! Total wind (actual + convective)
  3079)       real(kind=8)              :: uconv        ! Convective velocity
  3080)       real(kind=8)              :: uconv_prev   ! Previous convective velocity
  3081)       real(kind=8)              :: change       ! Difference in convective velocity
  3082)       integer                   :: icnt         ! Iteration counter
  3083)       !----- Aux. environment conditions. -------------------------------------------------!
  3084)       real(kind=8)              :: thetav_atm   ! Atmos. virtual pot. temp.     [        K]
  3085)       real(kind=8)              :: thetav_can   ! Canopy air virtual pot. temp. [        K]
  3086)       !----- External functions. ----------------------------------------------------------!
  3087)       real(kind=8), external    :: cbrt8              ! Cubic root
  3088)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3089) 
  3090) 
  3091)       !----- Find the variables common to both methods. -----------------------------------!
  3092)       thetav_atm = theta_atm * (1.d0 + epim18 * shv_atm)
  3093)       thetav_can = theta_can * (1.d0 + epim18 * shv_can)
  3094)       zstar      = zref - dheight
  3095)       zoz0m      = zstar / rough
  3096)       lnzoz0m    = log(zoz0m)
  3097)       zoz0h      = z0moz0h8 * zoz0m
  3098)       lnzoz0h    = log(zoz0h)
  3099)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3100) 
  3101) 
  3102) 
  3103)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3104)       !     Find the Bulk Richardson number.  For stable cases, and for L79 in both cases, !
  3105)       ! this will be the definitive RiB, whilst this is the first guess, which will be     !
  3106)       ! corrected by the convective velocity in the other unstable cases.                  !
  3107)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3108)       rib        = 2.d0 * grav8 * (zstar-rough) * (thetav_atm-thetav_can)                  &
  3109)                  / ( (thetav_atm+thetav_can) * uref * uref)
  3110)       stable     = thetav_atm >= thetav_can
  3111)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3112) 
  3113) 
  3114) 
  3115) 
  3116)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3117)       !    Correct the bulk Richardson number in case it's too stable.  We also define a   !
  3118)       ! stable case correction to bring down the stars other than ustar, so the flux       !
  3119)       ! doesn't increase for stabler cases (it remains constant).                          !
  3120)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3121)       if (rib > ribmax8) then
  3122)          uuse = sqrt(rib / ribmax8) * uref
  3123)          rib  = ribmax8
  3124)       else
  3125)          uuse = uref
  3126)       end if
  3127)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3128) 
  3129) 
  3130) 
  3131) 
  3132) 
  3133)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3134)       !     Here we find u* and the coefficient to find the other stars based on the       !
  3135)       ! chosen surface model.                                                              !
  3136)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3137)       select case (isfclyrm)
  3138)       case (0)
  3139)          !----- Use prescribed u* instead of calculating it. ------------------------------!
  3140)          ustar    = max(ustmin8,atm_ustar)
  3141)          kuoustar = vonk8 * uref / ustar
  3142)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3143) 
  3144) 
  3145)          !----- Find the dimensionless height. --------------------------------------------!
  3146)          zeta   = zoobukhov_ustar8(rib,zstar,rough,zoz0h,lnzoz0h,kuoustar,stable)
  3147)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3148) 
  3149) 
  3150)          !----- Find the dimensionless roughness. -----------------------------------------!
  3151)          zeta0m = rough * zeta / zstar
  3152)          zeta0h = z0hoz0m8 * zeta0m
  3153)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3154) 
  3155) 
  3156)          !----- Find the coefficient to scale the other stars. ----------------------------!
  3157)          c3    = vonk8                                                                     &
  3158)                / (tprandtl8 * (lnzoz0h - psih8(zeta,stable) + psih8(zeta0h,stable)))
  3159)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3160) 
  3161)       case (1)
  3162) 
  3163)          !----- Compute the a-square factor and the coefficient to find theta*. -----------!
  3164)          a2   = vonk8 * vonk8 / (lnzoz0m * lnzoz0m)
  3165)          c1   = a2 * uuse
  3166) 
  3167)          if (stable) then
  3168)             !----- Stable case ------------------------------------------------------------!
  3169) 
  3170)             fm = 1.d0 / (1.d0 + (2.d0 * bl798 * rib / sqrt(1.d0 + dl798 * rib)))
  3171)             fh = 1.d0 / (1.d0 + (3.d0 * bl798 * rib * sqrt(1.d0 + dl798 * rib)))
  3172) 
  3173)          else
  3174) 
  3175)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3176)             !     Unstable case.  The only difference from the original method is that we  !
  3177)             ! no longer assume z >> z0, so the "c" coefficient uses the full z/z0 term.    !
  3178)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3179)             ee = cbrt8(zoz0m) - 1.d0
  3180)             c2 = bl798 * a2 * ee * sqrt(ee * abs(rib))
  3181)             cm = csm8 * c2
  3182)             ch = csh8 * c2
  3183)             fm = (1.d0 - 2.d0 * bl798 * rib / (1.d0 + 2.d0 * cm))
  3184)             fh = (1.d0 - 3.d0 * bl798 * rib / (1.d0 + 3.d0 * ch))
  3185)          end if
  3186) 
  3187)          !----- Finding ustar, making sure it is not too small. ---------------------------!
  3188)          ustar = max(ustmin8,sqrt(c1 * uuse * fm))
  3189)          !----- Finding the coefficient to scale the other stars. -------------------------!
  3190)          c3 = c1 * fh / ustar
  3191)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3192) 
  3193)          !----- Compute zeta from u* and T* -----------------------------------------------!
  3194)          zeta = grav8 * vonk8 * c3 * (thetav_atm - thetav_can)                             &
  3195)               / (thetav_atm * ustar * ustar)
  3196) 
  3197) 
  3198)       case default
  3199)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3200)          ! 2. Here we use the model proposed by OD95, the standard for MM5, but with some  !
  3201)          !    terms that were computed in B71 (namely, the "0" terms), which prevent sin-  !
  3202)          !    gularities.                                                                  !
  3203)          !    However we know zeta, so zeta0 can be written as z0/z * zeta.                !
  3204)          ! 3. Here we use the model proposed by BH91, which is almost the same as the OD95 !
  3205)          !    method, except that the stable functions are computed in a more generic way. !
  3206)          !    BH91 claim that the oft-used approximation (-beta*zeta) can cause poor       !
  3207)          !    ventilation of the stable layer, leading to decoupling between the atmo-     !
  3208)          !    sphere and the canopy air space and excessive cooling                        !
  3209)          ! 4. Here we use a similar approach as in CLM04, excepth that the momentum flux   !
  3210)          !    gradient function for the unstable case for momentum is switched by a power  !
  3211)          !    of -1/6 (kind of the square of the heat one).  This is to guarantee that     !
  3212)          !    the psi function doesn't have local maxima/minima.                           !
  3213)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3214)          !----- Initialise uconv. ---------------------------------------------------------!
  3215)          uconv      = 0.0
  3216)          !----- Check if we need to go through the iterative process. ---------------------!
  3217)          if (stable) then
  3218)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3219)             !     Stable case, we don't need to find convective velocity, so we don't need !
  3220)             ! the iterative case.                                                          !
  3221)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3222)             !----- Find the dimensionless height. -----------------------------------------!
  3223)             zeta   = zoobukhov8(rib,zstar,rough,zoz0m,lnzoz0m,zoz0h,lnzoz0h,stable)
  3224)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3225) 
  3226) 
  3227)             !----- Find the dimensionless roughness. --------------------------------------!
  3228)             zeta0m = rough * zeta / zstar
  3229)             zeta0h = z0hoz0m8 * zeta0m
  3230)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3231) 
  3232) 
  3233) 
  3234)             !----- Find ustar, make sure it is not too small. -----------------------------!
  3235)             ustar = max (ustmin8, vonk8 * uuse                                             &
  3236)                                 / (lnzoz0m - psim8(zeta,stable) + psim8(zeta0m,stable)))
  3237)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3238) 
  3239) 
  3240) 
  3241)             !----- Find the coefficient to scale the other stars. -------------------------!
  3242)             c3    = vonk8                                                                  &
  3243)                   / (tprandtl8 * (lnzoz0h - psih8(zeta,stable) + psih8(zeta0h,stable)))
  3244)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3245)          else
  3246)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3247)             !    Unstable case.  Here we run a few iterations to make sure we correct the  !
  3248)             ! bulk Richardson number.  This is really a simple correction, so we don't     !
  3249)             ! need uconv to be totally in equilibrium.                                     !
  3250)             !------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3251)             unstable: do icnt=1,6
  3252)                !----- Update total winds. -------------------------------------------------!
  3253)                uconv_prev = uconv
  3254)                utotal     = sqrt(uuse*uuse + uconv_prev * uconv_prev)
  3255)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3256) 
  3257)                !----- Update the Bulk Richardson number. ----------------------------------!
  3258)                rib        = 2.d0 * grav8 * (zstar-rough) * (thetav_atm-thetav_can)         &
  3259)                           / ( (thetav_atm+thetav_can) * utotal * utotal)
  3260)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3261) 
  3262) 
  3263)                !----- Find the dimensionless height. --------------------------------------!
  3264)                zeta   = zoobukhov8(rib,zstar,rough,zoz0m,lnzoz0m,zoz0h,lnzoz0h,stable)
  3265)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3266) 
  3267) 
  3268)                !----- Find the dimensionless roughness. -----------------------------------!
  3269)                zeta0m = rough * zeta / zstar
  3270)                zeta0h = z0hoz0m8 * zeta0m
  3271)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3272) 
  3273) 
  3274)                !----- Find ustar, making sure it is not too small. ------------------------!
  3275)                ustar = max (ustmin8, vonk8 * uuse                                          &
  3276)                                    / (lnzoz0m - psim8(zeta,stable) + psim8(zeta0m,stable)))
  3277)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3278) 
  3279) 
  3280)                !----- Find the coefficient to scale the other stars. ----------------------!
  3281)                c3    = vonk8                                                               &
  3282)                      / (tprandtl8 * (lnzoz0h - psih8(zeta,stable) + psih8(zeta0h,stable)))
  3283)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3284) 
  3285) 
  3286)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3287)                !     Use potential virtual temperature here because convection is related  !
  3288)                ! to buoyancy.                                                              !
  3289)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3290)                tstar  = c3 * (thetav_atm - thetav_can )
  3291)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3292) 
  3293) 
  3294)                !----- Estimate the convective velocity. -----------------------------------!
  3295)                uconv = vertical_vel_flux8(zeta,ustar) / ustar
  3296)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3297) 
  3298) 
  3299)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3300)                !     We are only after a rough estimate of this velocity, so if the        !
  3301)                ! difference is less than the RK4 tolerance, then it's enough.              !
  3302)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3303)                change = 2.d0 * abs(uconv-uconv_prev) / (abs(uconv) + abs(uconv_prev))
  3304)                if (change < rk4eps) exit unstable
  3305)                !---------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3306)             end do unstable
  3307)          end if
  3308)          !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3309)       end select
  3310)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3311) 
  3312) 
  3313) 
  3314)       !----- Compute the other scales. ----------------------------------------------------!
  3315)       qstar = c3 * (shv_atm      - shv_can      )
  3316)       tstar = c3 * (theta_atm    - theta_can    )
  3317)       estar = c3 * (enthalpy_atm - enthalpy_can )
  3318)       cstar = c3 * (co2_atm      - co2_can      )
  3319)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3320) 
  3321) 
  3322) 
  3323)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3324)       !    Compute the bare ground conductance.  This equation is similar to the original, !
  3325)       ! except that we don't assume the ratio between the gradient and the characteristic  !
  3326)       ! scale to be 0.2; instead we use the actual ratio that is computed here.            !
  3327)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3328)       ggbare = c3 * ustar
  3329)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3330) 
  3331)       return
  3332)    end subroutine ed_stars8

  3343)    !    This function determines the wind at a given height, given that the stars are al-  !
  3344)    ! ready known, as well as the Richardson number and the zetas.                          !
  3345)    !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3346)    real(kind=4) function reduced_wind(ustar,zeta,rib,zref,dheight,height,rough)
  3347)       use consts_coms    , only : vonk     ! ! intent(in)
  3348)       use canopy_air_coms, only : isfclyrm & ! intent(in)
  3349)                                 , bl79     & ! intent(in)
  3350)                                 , csm      & ! intent(in)
  3351)                                 , dl79     & ! intent(in)
  3352)                                 , ugbmin   & ! intent(in)
  3353)                                 , psim     ! ! function
  3354)       implicit none
  3355)       !----- Arguments. -------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3356)       real(kind=4), intent(in) :: ustar     ! Friction velocity                   [    m/s]
  3357)       real(kind=4), intent(in) :: zeta      ! Normalised height                   [    ---]
  3358)       real(kind=4), intent(in) :: rib       ! Bulk Richardson number              [    ---]
  3359)       real(kind=4), intent(in) :: zref      ! Reference height                    [      m]
  3360)       real(kind=4), intent(in) :: dheight   ! Displacement height                 [      m]
  3361)       real(kind=4), intent(in) :: height    ! Height to determine the red. wind   [      m]
  3362)       real(kind=4), intent(in) :: rough     ! Roughness scale                     [      m]
  3363)       !----- Local variables. -------------------------------------------------------------!
  3364)       logical                  :: stable    ! Canopy air space is stable          [    T|F]
  3365)       real(kind=4)             :: zetah     ! Zeta for h=height                   [    ---]
  3366)       real(kind=4)             :: zeta0     ! Zeta for h=rough                    [    ---]
  3367)       real(kind=4)             :: hoz0      ! ((h-dheight)/z0)                    [    ---]
  3368)       real(kind=4)             :: lnhoz0    ! ln ((h-dheight)/z0)                 [    ---]
  3369)       real(kind=4)             :: a2        ! Drag coeff. in neutral conditions
  3370)       real(kind=4)             :: fm        ! Stability parameter for momentum
  3371)       real(kind=4)             :: c2        ! Part of the c coefficient.
  3372)       real(kind=4)             :: cm        ! c coefficient times |Rib|^1/2
  3373)       real(kind=4)             :: ee        ! (z/z0)^1/3 -1. for eqn. 20 (L79)
  3374)       !----- External functions. ----------------------------------------------------------!
  3375)       real(kind=4), external   :: cbrt      ! Cubic root
  3376)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3377) 
  3378) 
  3379) 
  3380)       !----- Define whether the layer is stable or not. -----------------------------------!
  3381)       stable    = rib >= 0.
  3382)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3383) 
  3384) 
  3385) 
  3386)       !----- Find the log for the log-height interpolation of wind. -----------------------!
  3387)       hoz0      = (height - dheight)/rough
  3388)       lnhoz0    = log(hoz0)
  3389)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3390) 
  3391) 
  3392) 
  3393)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3394)       !     The wind at a given height is found by using the same definition of wind speed !
  3395)       ! at a given height.                                                                 !
  3396)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3397)       select case (isfclyrm)
  3398)       case (1) !---- Louis (1979) method. -------------------------------------------------!
  3399) 
  3400)          !----- Compute the a-square factor and the coefficient to find theta*. -----------!
  3401)          a2   = vonk * vonk / (lnhoz0 * lnhoz0)
  3402) 
  3403)          if (stable) then
  3404)             !----- Stable case ------------------------------------------------------------!

  3757)    !     This sub-routine computes the aerodynamic conductance between leaf and canopy     !
  3758)    ! air space for both heat and water vapour, based on:                                   !
  3759)    !                                                                                       !

  3763)    ! M08 - Monteith, J. L., M. H. Unsworth, 2008. Principles of Environmental Physics,     !
  3764)    !       3rd. edition, Academic Press, Amsterdam, 418pp.  (Mostly Chapter 10).           !
  3765)    !                                                                                       !

  3767)    ! - gbh is in J/(K m2 s), and                                                           !

  3769)    !---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3770)    subroutine leaf_aerodynamic_conductances(ipft,veg_wind,leaf_temp,can_temp               &
  3771)                                            ,can_rhos,can_cp,leaf_gbh,leaf_gbw)
  3772)       use pft_coms       , only : leaf_width   ! ! intent(in)
  3773)       use canopy_air_coms, only : aflat_lami   & ! intent(in)
  3774)                                 , nflat_lami   & ! intent(in)
  3775)                                 , aflat_turb   & ! intent(in)
  3776)                                 , nflat_turb   & ! intent(in)
  3777)                                 , bflat_lami   & ! intent(in)
  3778)                                 , mflat_lami   & ! intent(in)
  3779)                                 , bflat_turb   & ! intent(in)
  3780)                                 , mflat_turb   & ! intent(in)
  3781)                                 , gbhmos_min   ! ! intent(in)
  3782)       use consts_coms    , only : t00          & ! intent(in)
  3783)                                 , grav         & ! intent(in)
  3784)                                 , kin_visc0    & ! intent(in)
  3785)                                 , dkin_visc    & ! intent(in)
  3786)                                 , th_diff0     & ! intent(in)
  3787)                                 , dth_diff     ! ! intent(in)
  3788)       use physiology_coms, only : gbh_2_gbw    ! ! intent(in)
  3789)       implicit none
  3790)       !----- Arguments. -------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3791)       integer                      :: ipft            ! Plant functional type   [      ---]
  3792)       real(kind=4)   , intent(in)  :: veg_wind        ! Wind at cohort height   [      m/s]
  3793)       real(kind=4)   , intent(in)  :: leaf_temp       ! Leaf temperature        [        K]
  3794)       real(kind=4)   , intent(in)  :: can_temp        ! Canopy air temperature  [        K]
  3795)       real(kind=4)   , intent(in)  :: can_rhos        ! Canopy air density      [    kg/m�]
  3796)       real(kind=4)   , intent(in)  :: can_cp          ! Canopy air spec. heat   [   J/kg/K]
  3797)       real(kind=4)   , intent(out) :: leaf_gbh        ! Heat  conductance       [ J/K/m�/s]
  3798)       real(kind=4)   , intent(out) :: leaf_gbw        ! Water conductance       [  kg/m�/s]
  3799)       !----- Local variables. -------------------------------------------------------------!
  3800)       real(kind=4)                 :: lwidth          ! Leaf width              [        m]
  3801)       real(kind=4)                 :: kin_visc        ! Kinematic viscosity     [     m�/s]
  3802)       real(kind=4)                 :: th_diff         ! Kinematic viscosity     [     m�/s]
  3803)       real(kind=4)                 :: th_expan        ! Thermal expansion       [      1/K]
  3804)       real(kind=4)                 :: gr_coeff        ! grav*th_expan/kin_visc� [   1/K/m�]
  3805)       real(kind=4)                 :: grashof         ! Grashof number          [      ---]
  3806)       real(kind=4)                 :: reynolds        ! Reynolds number         [      ---]
  3807)       real(kind=4)                 :: nusselt_lami    ! Nusselt number (laminar)[      ---]
  3808)       real(kind=4)                 :: nusselt_turb    ! Nusselt number (turb.)  [      ---]
  3809)       real(kind=4)                 :: nusselt         ! Nusselt number          [      ---]
  3810)       real(kind=4)                 :: forced_gbh_mos  ! Forced convection cond. [      m/s]
  3811)       real(kind=4)                 :: free_gbh_mos    ! Free convection cond.   [      m/s]
  3812)       real(kind=4)                 :: gbh_mos         ! Total convection cond.  [      m/s]
  3813)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3814) 
  3815) 
  3816)       !----- Save the leaf width of this PFT. ---------------------------------------------!
  3817)       lwidth = leaf_width(ipft)
  3818)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3819) 
  3820) 
  3821) 
  3822)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3823)       !     Compute kinematic viscosity, thermal diffusivity, and expansion coefficient as !
  3824)       ! functions of temperature.  Here we use the canopy air space temperature because    !
  3825)       ! this is the representative temperature of the fluid.                               !
  3826)       !                                                                                    !
  3827)       !     Kinematic viscosity and thermal diffusivity are determined from MU08, see      !
  3828)       ! discussion on page 32.  Thermal expansion is assumed to be of an ideal gas (1/T),  !
  3829)       ! like in Dufour and van Mieghem (1975), for example.                                !
  3830)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3831)       !----- Check for singularity when computing thermal expansion. ----------------------!
  3832)       th_expan = 1.0 / can_temp
  3833)       !----- kin_visc and th_diff are assumed linear functions of temperature. ------------!
  3834)       kin_visc = kin_visc0 * ( 1.0 + dkin_visc * ( can_temp - t00 ) )
  3835)       th_diff  = th_diff0  * ( 1.0 + dth_diff  * ( can_temp - t00 ) )
  3836)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3837)       !    Grashof coefficient (a*g/nu�) in MU08's equation 10.8.                          !
  3838)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3839)       gr_coeff = th_expan * grav  / ( kin_visc * kin_visc )
  3840)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3841) 
  3842) 
  3843)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3844)       !     Find the conductance, in m/s, associated with forced convection.               !
  3845)       !------------------------------------------------------------------------------------!
  3846)       !----- 1. Compute the Reynolds number. ----------------------------------------------!
  3847)       reynolds        = veg_wind * lwidth / th_diff
  3848)       !----- 2. Compute the Nusselt number for both the laminar and turbulent case. -------!
  3849)       nusselt_lami    = aflat_lami * reynolds ** nflat_lami
  3850)       nusselt_turb    = aflat_turb * reynolds ** nflat_turb
  3851)       !----- 3. The right Nusselt number is the largest. ----------------------------------!
  3852)       nusselt         = max(nusselt_lami,nusselt_turb)
